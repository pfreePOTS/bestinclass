## 1. The P&L Reconciliation Gap

The first major finding from building the unified model is a significant discrepancy between the labor costs tracked in your systems and the labor costs reported on the P&L.

- **Total Labor on P&L:** $3,171,690 *(Salaries + Payroll Taxes + Subcontractors + Partner GP)*
- **Total Labor in Our Model:** $2,504,596 *(All W-2 staff + All known contractors + Partner GP)*
- **The Gap:** **$667,093**

### Where is the missing $667K?
The gap is almost entirely in the Subcontractor COGS line. The P&L reports **$1,044,424** in subcontractor expense. Our model—which includes Basim, David, Blue Pisces, and all the Help Desk contractors (George, AJ, Omar, Jeremy, Dare, Peal)—only accounts for **$882,275** in contractor COGS.

There is roughly **$162,000** in subcontractor expense on the P&L that is not accounted for in the recurring expenses tracker. This likely represents one-off project contractors, part-year contractors not listed, or timing differences in accounting. 

*Note: For the margin analysis below, we use the modeled costs so we can assign them to specific service lines, but be aware that the true P&L costs are higher, meaning the actual margins are slightly worse than modeled here.*

## 3. The New Profit Leakage Map

If the gross margins are actually hitting Best-in-Class targets (50.2% blended vs 44% target), why is the bottom-line EBITDA only 4.2% instead of the 18.5% target?

**The profit leakage is entirely in SG&A (Overhead).**

Our model reveals that while your delivery teams (COGS) are highly efficient, the overhead structure built to support them is consuming all the profit.

### SG&A Breakdown vs BIC Targets

| SG&A Category | Modeled Cost | % of Revenue | BIC Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Service Leadership** | $359,737 | 7.0% | *(Included in Total)* |
| **Sales & Marketing** | $474,824 | 9.2% | 5.4% |
| **Operations / G&A** | $232,654 | 4.5% | *(Included in Total)* |
| **Non-Labor SG&A (est)** | $573,438 | 11.1% | *(Included in Total)* |
| **TOTAL SG&A** | **$1,640,653** | **31.7%** | **27.4%** |

### The Three Sources of Profit Leakage (Updated)

1. **Software Sold Below Cost (Negative Margin):** You are losing **$179,494** in profit by paying vendors more than you charge clients. *(Immediate fix: Update ConnectWise pricing).*
2. **Sales & Marketing Overspend:** You are spending 9.2% of revenue on S&M against a BIC target of 5.4%. That is **$196,000** in excess spend. This would be acceptable if it were driving high growth, but Managed Services revenue declined 16.2% last year. The ROI is negative.
3. **Leadership Overhead:** The Service Leadership team (Calkins, Walsh, Froio's non-billable time) costs **$359,737** annually. This is a massive investment in management capacity for a $5M business. 

### The Core Strategic Question

The data tells a very clear story: **You have built the management and overhead structure of a $10M+ MSP, but you only have $5.17M in revenue to support it.**

Your delivery teams (Help Desk and Projects) are operating at peak efficiency (61% and 54% gross margins). They cannot squeeze out any more profit. The only two paths to reaching the 18.5% EBITDA target are:

**Path A (Grow into the overhead):** The $474K Sales & Marketing engine must reverse the MS revenue decline and rapidly add $2M+ in new Managed Services agreements to dilute the leadership overhead.
**Path B (Shrink the overhead):** If rapid MS growth is not achievable, the $1.64M SG&A burden must be reduced to align with the current $5.17M revenue reality.
