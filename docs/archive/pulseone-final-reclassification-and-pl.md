## Part 6: Profit Leakage Analysis

### Where Is PulseOne Losing Margin vs. BIC?

| Issue | Annual Impact | Root Cause | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gross margin 11.2 pts below BIC** | **$579,165** | Service delivery labor cost too high relative to revenue; pricing may be insufficient | **Critical** |
| **Recurring revenue only 34.8% of total** | Structural | Heavy dependence on project/non-recurring revenue; lower margin quality | **Critical** |
| **SG&A 4.3 pts above BIC** | $222,000 | Sales/marketing overhead elevated; partner comp is 8.2% of revenue | **High** |
| **Net income 10.5 pts below BIC** | $543,000 | Combination of gross margin gap + SG&A gap | **High** |
| **Normalized EBITDA 4.6 pts below BIC** | $238,000 | After normalizing partner comp, still below BIC | **High** |

### The Core Equation

At $5.17M revenue, closing the gross margin gap from 38.8% to 50% requires either:

**Option A — Revenue Growth (holding costs flat):** Add ~$1.87M in revenue at 50%+ gross margin (i.e., managed services agreements) to bring the blended margin to 50%.

**Option B — Cost Reduction (holding revenue flat):** Reduce service delivery labor cost by ~$579,000/year — approximately 4–5 FTE equivalents at average burdened cost, or equivalent contractor reduction.

**Option C — Pricing Improvement:** Increase managed services pricing by ~15–20% across the agreement base to improve gross margin without headcount changes.

**Option D — Mix Shift:** Convert project/non-recurring revenue to managed services agreements. Each $100K of project revenue converted to managed services at 50% gross margin adds $50K in gross profit.

The most realistic path is a combination of Options C and D — pricing discipline and recurring revenue growth — which is precisely what the Service Leadership BIC framework recommends.
