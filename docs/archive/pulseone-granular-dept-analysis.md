## 3. Departmental Deep Dives

### A. Marketing (9.1% vs 3.0% Target) — $314K Overspend
This is the most structurally misaligned department in the business. You are spending three times the BIC target on Marketing.
- **Major Costs:** ADP Dept 200 W-2 Staff ($236K), Paul Partner Comp ($141K), Advertising ($51K).
- **Strategic Issue:** You are spending nearly half a million dollars on Marketing, but only $69K on Sales. This ratio is inverted. BIC firms invest heavily in quota-carrying Sales capacity and keep Marketing lean. Furthermore, this $469K Marketing investment did not prevent a 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue.

### B. G&A (18.9% vs 12.5% Target) — $328K Overspend
G&A is carrying the heaviest burden in the company.
- **Major Costs:** Rod Partner Comp ($141K), Charlie Partner Comp ($141K), Computer & Internet Expenses ($263K), Insurance ($129K).
- **Strategic Issue:** Between Rod, Charlie, and the $263K in IT/SaaS tool sprawl, the fixed G&A burden is too heavy for a $5M revenue base. The Computer & Internet line item ($263K) needs an immediate vendor audit — this is often where unused SaaS licenses and legacy IT tools hide.

### C. Service Leadership (7.5% vs 5.5% Target) — $102K Overspend
- **Major Costs:** Calkins ($164K burdened), Walsh 80% ($141K burdened), Froio 40% ($49K burdened), Training ($23K).
- **Strategic Issue:** This is a slight over-investment, but it is acceptable *if* it results in high delivery margins. Since your delivery gross margin is 49.3% (beating the BIC target), this leadership team is doing its job effectively. This is not the primary source of profit leakage.

### D. Sales (1.3% vs 4.0% Target) — $137K Underspend
- **Major Costs:** RepScheduler CRM ($39K), Walsh 20% ($23K).
- **Strategic Issue:** You have almost no dedicated Sales capacity. You are relying entirely on Marketing to drive revenue. This explains why growth has stalled.

## 4. The Path to 18.5% EBITDA

Your Normalized EBITDA is currently **12.5%** ($648K). 
*(Note: This is higher than the 4.2% reported on the P&L because we have not accounted for the $162K in missing contractor COGS, and we have normalized some non-operating expenses. But the structural gap remains).*

To close the gap to the 18.5% BIC target, you must address the departmental imbalances:

1. **Rebalance Sales & Marketing:** You are spending $539K combined on Sales and Marketing, which is roughly correct for your size. But it is 87% Marketing / 13% Sales. You need to shift $200K+ of that capacity from Marketing into quota-carrying Sales to drive actual revenue growth.
2. **Audit G&A Tool Sprawl:** Pull the detailed general ledger for the $263,953 "Computer and Internet Expenses" line. Find the unused SaaS licenses and redundant IT tools and cut them.
3. **Fix Software Pricing:** The $179K loss on software resale is still the fastest, easiest cash to recover.
