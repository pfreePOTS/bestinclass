## The Gross Margin Gap: Root Cause Analysis

At 37.4% corrected gross margin vs. the BIC target of 50%, PulseOne has a **12.6-point gap representing $653,503 in annual margin leakage** at current revenue levels.

### What Is Driving the Gap

| Driver | Estimated Impact | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| **Total service delivery labor too high relative to revenue** | Primary driver | Structural — pricing and/or utilization |
| **Recurring revenue only ~35% of total** | Secondary driver | Mix — project revenue carries lower margin |
| **Upwork contractors at fixed/hourly rates not fully recovered in billing** | Tertiary driver | Pricing — contractor cost not fully passed through |

### The Math on Service Delivery Labor

Total service delivery labor (all sources) as a percentage of revenue:

| Component | Amount | % of Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| W-2 service delivery (burdened) | ~$794,000 | 15.4% |
| Upwork contractors (CY 2025) | ~$259,000 | 5.0% |
| Other contractors in QB COGS (Daniyal 80%, Robyn, after-hours) | ~$79,000 | 1.5% |
| QB Consulting Services – Cost (remaining) | ~$706,000 | 13.7% |
| **Total Service Delivery Labor** | **~$1,838,000** | **~35.6%** |

BIC MSPs run total service delivery labor at **25–30% of revenue**. PulseOne is running at approximately **35.6%** — roughly **6–10 points above BIC**. At $5.17M revenue, closing that gap to 30% would free up **$285,000–$544,000** in gross profit annually.
