## A. Objective

To present the definitive CY 2025 Profit & Loss statement for PulseOne, mapped to the four Service Leadership business models (Managed Services, Comprehensive Services, True Projects, Products & Licensing). 

**Version 6.0 Update:** This version restructures the segments to properly match revenue and cost for recurring software and hosting. "Agreement Licenses" revenue and cost have been moved out of Managed Services and into a consolidated **Products & Licensing** segment. This corrects the prior mismatch where product costs were sitting in the Products segment while their corresponding revenue was bundled into MS agreements.

## C. Methodology & Key Constraints

1. **Total Cost Constraint:** The total costs in this model ($4,950,980) match the QuickBooks total costs exactly. No costs were invented, and no costs were dropped.
2. **Revenue Segmentation:**
   - **Managed Services:** Only "IT Managed Services" QB line (pure service delivery).
   - **Comprehensive Services:** QuickBase ARC/Bunzl PM staffing revenue minus the $133K Adobe software resale portion.
   - **True Projects:** QuickBase named projects (AD Migration, etc.).
   - **Products & Licensing:** The remainder of all revenue, encompassing hardware, software licensing (M365, Barracuda, Adobe, AvePoint), and cloud hosting (Colo, Azure, AWS).
3. **Actual Labor Costs:** W-2 labor uses actual gross pay. Offshore labor uses actual 2025 payments from Payoneer ($172,842) and Upwork ($299,924).
4. **Payroll Burden:** Payroll taxes ($296,256) and payroll expenses ($14,620) are allocated proportionally across the P&L based on the destination of the W-2 labor that generated them.

## H. Findings & Best in Class Alignment

### 1. Managed Services (60.2% GM) — *Above Benchmark*
By isolating the pure service delivery component (removing Agreement Licenses revenue and cost), the MS segment operates at an exceptional 60.2% gross margin. This reflects a highly efficient offshore/outsourced delivery model (George, Omar, Dare, Charlesdoone) that keeps labor costs well below industry averages.

### 2. Comprehensive Services (34.7% GM) — *Slightly Below Benchmark*
After removing the $133K Adobe software pass-through from the ARC revenue, the pure staffing augmentation model operates at 34.7% margin. This is slightly below the ~40% BiC target, suggesting that the markup on the Blue Pisces and independent contractors (Ruben, Falkenberg, Basim, BJ) could be optimized.

### 3. True Projects (23.7% GM) — *Below Benchmark*
While QuickBase shows True Projects operating at a 52.5% direct margin (subcontractors only), the fully burdened margin drops to 23.7% once the internal W-2 engineering labor (Calkins, Froio, Hagen) and Cost of Delivery overhead (Harris, Walsh) are applied. The BiC target is 40%. PulseOne is not fully pricing its internal engineering and PM overhead into project statements of work.

### 4. Products & Licensing (36.2% GM) — *Above Benchmark*
The consolidated products segment is highly profitable, driven by excellent margins on Hardware (52.2%) and Hosting/Cloud (51.0%). Software Licensing (28.7%) is above the BiC minimum (24%) but is dragged down by the near-zero margin Adobe renewals for ARC Research.

## I. Strategic Conclusion

PulseOne's 6.4% EBITDA is below the BiC target of 12-18% not because of gross margin failures in its core recurring businesses (MS and Products are both above benchmark), but because of two structural issues:
1. **Project Underpricing:** True Projects are absorbing heavy internal W-2 labor costs that are not being fully recovered in the SOW pricing, resulting in a 23.7% GM.
2. **SG&A Bloat:** Total SG&A is 36.7% of revenue, well above the ~30% BiC target. The G&A labor burden (Partners + Admin/Ops) is consuming the gross profit generated by the MS engine.
