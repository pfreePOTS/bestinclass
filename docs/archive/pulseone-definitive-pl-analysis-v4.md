## A. Objective

Previous versions of the P&L applied top-down reclassifications but relied on estimates for segment-level margins. This version rebuilds the CY 2025 P&L **from the bottom up**, allocating every W-2 employee, Upwork contractor, Expense Tracker vendor, and SaaS tool to specific business segments based on confirmed data.

The goal is to provide an exact, mathematically reconciled view of where PulseOne's profit leakage is occurring, specifically addressing the 11.2-point gap to the Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) EBITDA benchmark.

## B. The Core Finding: The Managed Services Illusion

Previous top-down models suggested Managed Services (MS) was operating at a highly profitable 56% Gross Margin, while Projects were dragging the company down. **The bottom-up build proves this was an illusion.**

When you properly allocate the $1.08M in hardware/software COGS and fully burden the MS labor pool with payroll taxes and dedicated tools, **Managed Services is operating at 41.7% Gross Margin** — nearly 11 points below the BiC target of 52.4%. 

PulseOne does not have a "project problem" masking a healthy MS business. PulseOne has a structural margin problem across *both* primary service lines.

## F. The $578K Gap Attribution (Definitive)

With the bottom-up model complete, we can now definitively attribute the $578K profit gap:

1. **Managed Services Margin Leakage: ~$288,000 (50% of gap)**
   *   MS is running at 41.7% GM instead of 52.4%. On $2.7M in revenue, this 10.7-point miss is the single largest driver of the profit gap.
   *   *Root cause:* High blended labor rates (senior W-2s doing HD) and unoptimized software licensing costs.

2. **Project Margin Leakage: ~$221,000 (38% of gap)**
   *   Projects are running at 31.2% GM instead of 50.0%.
   *   *Root cause:* Heavy reliance on subcontractor pass-throughs without sufficient markup, plus unbilled W-2 project management time.

3. **Sales & Marketing Overspend: ~$240,000 (41% of gap)**
   *   S&M is 10.1% of revenue vs the 5.4% BiC target.
   *   *Root cause:* Carrying $519K in S&M costs (Wiggins, Carey, contractors) while experiencing a 16.2% revenue decline.

4. **G&A Efficiency Offset: +$171,000 (Helps close the gap)**
   *   PulseOne runs incredibly lean on G&A (14.2% vs 22.6% BiC), which partially masks the bleeding in COGS and S&M.

## G. Recommended Actions

1. **Reprice the Managed Services Base (Priority 1):** The MS book of business is structurally unprofitable. A 10-15% rate increase on renewals is mandatory to offset the $862K software licensing burden and the blended labor rates.
2. **Audit Project Subcontractor Markups (Priority 1):** Pull the invoices for the $389K in project subcontractors. If these are being marked up less than 50% (e.g., 20-30% pass-through), PulseOne will never hit the 50% project margin target.
3. **Right-Size Sales & Marketing (Priority 2):** $519K is too much to spend on S&M for a $5M business that is shrinking. Evaluate the ROI of the W-2 BizDev roles (Wiggins/Carey) and the contractor spend.
4. **Lock the QuickBooks Reclassification (Priority 3):** Hand this document to the bookkeeper. The $1,010,174 in W-2 gross pay and $197,693 in Upwork costs must be formally moved from SG&A to COGS to make this reality visible in the monthly financials.
