## Key Observations

1. **Service Delivery is essentially at BiC benchmark.** The Service Multiple of Labor is 2.76x against a 2.8x target. The team is right-sized for current revenue.

2. **Sales & Marketing is the critical overspend.** At 14.6% of revenue vs. 5.4% BiC target, this department is consuming $46,531/month — approximately $29,000/month more than benchmark would allow ($17,226 at 5.4%).

3. **G&A is actually UNDER the BiC benchmark** at 15.2% vs. 22.6%. This is because partner comp ($35,250/month) is modest relative to revenue, and operations staff is lean.

4. **The net P&L shows $29,443/month profit at $319K revenue** — but this assumes all revenue is collected and all costs are captured. The April Profitability Sheet showed -$7.5K, suggesting approximately $37K in costs this model may not capture (additional overhead, one-time items, or timing differences).

5. **The 4 new sales reps ($8K/month)** have a 90-day window to prove value before converting to W-2. If they produce no revenue by August, they represent pure incremental loss.
