## Section 9: Key Findings from Client Profitability Research

The parallel session's client analysis identified the following actionable findings:

1. **Bottom-Tier Clients Consuming Disproportionate Resources:** Eight clients generating less than $2,000/month each were consuming 94 hours of technical labor monthly. Shedding these clients frees labor capacity that enables staff reductions without breaking service delivery for profitable accounts.

2. **ITMS Billing Leakage:** Approximately $43,000 in project-scope work was delivered to ITMS clients over 5 months without being billed as project work. Enforcing strict boundaries between covered MS and billable project work would recover ~$8,600/month in high-margin revenue.

3. **IT Assessment Conversion Failure:** 28 IT Assessments were sold for $72K, but converted into only $20,723 in true Managed Services revenue. The assessments are being used to sell one-time hardware and projects rather than recurring agreements.

4. **Bunzl Concentration Risk:** The Bunzl family represents 56% of MS Agreement revenue ($84,823 of $151,644). Any disruption to this relationship would be existential.
