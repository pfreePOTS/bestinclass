## Section 6: The Three Structural Problems (Integrated with Client Research)

### Problem 1: Managed Services Gross Margin is Destroyed by Overstaffing

**The Evidence:**
- MS Revenue: $156,000/month
- MS COGS (labor + software + tools): $106,254/month
- **MS Gross Margin: 31.9%** (BiC target: 55-60% for pure MS)

**Root Cause:** PulseOne is carrying $63,346/month in MS labor (W-2 + outsourced) against a client base that generates only 3,438 tickets in 5 months (~688 tickets/month). The Service Multiple of Labor is only **2.46x** ($156K revenue / $63.3K labor) — below the 2.8x BiC target.

**Client Profitability Research Confirms:** The bottom 10 accounts consume 94 hours/month at effective rates of $54-$99/hour — well below the $150+ BiC threshold. These accounts are consuming technician capacity that could serve profitable clients.

**The Fix (from Restructuring Plan):**
- Shed 8 unprofitable bottom-tier clients (-$6,185/mo revenue, +94 hrs freed)
- Eliminate Charlesdoone Castro ($2,716/mo — dedicated to churned client)
- Eliminate Breanna Copeland ($2,329/mo — hours absorbed by freed capacity)
- Reduce coverage hours: eliminate Dare Olusanjo ($4,333/mo), reduce George ($1,842/mo)
- **Net MS COGS reduction: $11,220/month**
- **Net revenue loss: $6,185/month**
- **Net improvement: $5,035/month**
- **New MS GM: 35.5%** (still below BiC, but moving in right direction)

---

### Problem 2: Projects are Catastrophically Unprofitable

**The Evidence:**
- Project Revenue (midpoint): $50,000/month
- Project COGS (fully loaded): $37,776/month
- **Project Gross Margin: 24.4%** (BiC target: 40-50% for projects)
- At the LOW scenario ($25K revenue): **Projects lose $12,776/month (-51.1% margin)**

**Root Cause:** PulseOne carries $37,776/month in fixed project labor capacity regardless of project volume. The project tracking system hides this because it only shows BILL.com subcontractor COGS, not W-2/Upwork labor. The 2025 analysis proved this: projects showed 47% cash margin but were actually deeply negative when W-2 time was included.

**Sales Research Confirms:** The sales team is NOT producing project pipeline. eanzalone dropped 75% in production. CWiggins collapsed 90%. IT Assessments convert at 46% but yield only $20K in MS revenue — they're generating hardware sales, not recurring services or sustained project work.

**The Fix (from Restructuring Plan):**
- Reduce Roe Tech by 40% ($3,320/mo savings)
- Reduce My IT Services by 50% ($3,640/mo savings)
- Reduce Vincent Williams by 20% ($1,820/mo savings)
- Reduce Matt Barnett by 15% ($1,119/mo savings)
- **Total Project COGS reduction: $9,899/month**
- **New Project COGS: $27,877/month**
- At $50K revenue: **New Project GM: 44.2%** (at BiC target)
- At $25K revenue: **Still losing $2,877/month** (but manageable)

### Problem 3: Sales & Marketing is 4x the BiC Target

**The Evidence:**
- Combined S&M: $62,468/month (21.9% of revenue)
- BiC target: 5-7% of revenue ($14,200-$19,900)
- **Overspend: $42,500-$48,200/month ($510K-$578K/year)**

**Root Cause:** PulseOne is spending as if it's a $1M/month company trying to grow, but it's actually a $284K/month company trying to survive. The sales function has produced almost zero net-new MS Agreement revenue:
- Total new MS Agreement revenue in ALL of 2025: $13,990

- **Annual MS sales production: $14K-$19K against $600K in annual sales spend**
- **ROI on Sales: 3% return**

**The Fix:**
- Immediate: Cut Marcello + Gianpaolo ($5,807/mo) — confirmed zero pipeline
- 90-day clock: Hinkley ($10K) + 4 new reps ($8K) must produce measurable pipeline by August
- Structural: Walsh presales allocation (30% = $3,788/mo) is expensive for a company with $14K/year in new MS sales
- **Minimum immediate savings: $5,807/month**

## Section 10: The Fundamental Truth

**PulseOne cannot cut its way to profitability at $284K/month revenue.** Even with maximum restructuring, you are still slightly cash-negative. The math only works if:

1. **Revenue recovers to $334K+/month** (the High scenario), OR
2. **Partner compensation is temporarily reduced** ($35K/month is 12.3% of revenue — unsustainable at this scale), OR
3. **Both Tier 1+2 cuts are executed AND the sales team produces $50K+/month in incremental revenue within 90 days**

The decision framework is:
- **If you believe the sales team will produce:** Execute Tier 1+2 now, give Hinkley 90 days, monitor monthly
- **If you need immediate survival:** Execute all tiers, cut partner comp temporarily, operate at minimum viable scale until revenue recovers
- **The hybrid path:** Execute Tier 1+2 immediately, give sales 60 days (not 90), and prepare Tier 3+4 as pre-approved contingencies

## Section 13: What Must Be True for PulseOne to Reach BiC

To achieve BiC-level performance (18.3% EBITDA) at the current revenue level ($284K/month):
- **Required EBITDA:** $52,000/month
- **Required Total OpEx:** $28,170/month (currently $129,638 — requires 78% reduction)
- **This is mathematically impossible** at $284K revenue with the current cost structure

To achieve BiC at a realistic OpEx level (after all restructuring = $90,667):
- **Required Revenue:** $520,000/month ($6.2M annual)
- **This requires nearly doubling current revenue**

**The realistic path to BiC:** Grow revenue to $400K+/month while holding the post-restructuring cost base. At $400K:
- GM: ~$140K (35%)
- OpEx: $90K
- EBITDA: $50K (12.5%) — approaching BiC
