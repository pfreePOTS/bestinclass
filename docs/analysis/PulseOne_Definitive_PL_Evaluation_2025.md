## A. Objective

This document provides a comprehensive evaluation of PulseOne's 2025 Profit & Loss (P&L) performance by service line and department. The objective is to identify the root causes of the low profit margins experienced in 2025, isolate unresolved issues and data gaps, and provide hindsight recommendations for structural fixes. 

This analysis is grounded strictly in the Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) framework and incorporates all owner-confirmed data corrections, including entity separation (RepScheduler and PulseOne Communications) and Fair Market Value (FMV) partner compensation treatment [1] [2].

## C. Current Understanding: The True 2025 Baseline

PulseOne's original QuickBooks P&L presented a distorted view of the business due to severe chart-of-accounts misclassifications. Service delivery labor (W-2 and contractor) and service delivery tools were commingled with SG&A expenses, creating an artificially high gross margin and an artificially high SG&A burden [3].

Furthermore, costs belonging to separate entities (RepScheduler and PulseOne Communications) were burdening the PulseOne operating baseline. Once these ~$132,830 in non-operating costs are removed, and service delivery costs are correctly shifted to COGS, the true operating reality emerges [4].

## E. Root Causes of Low Profit Margins in 2025

The forensic analysis isolates the $507K operating profit gap into specific, fixable structural failures:

1.  **Project Margin Collapse (~$193,000 Leakage):** The failure to include fully-loaded internal W-2 labor costs in project quotes meant PulseOne was subsidizing client projects with its own engineering payroll. The segment operated at a **loss**.
2.  **Comprehensive Margin Below Target (~$66,000 Leakage):** The Comprehensive segment at 35.2% is 4.8 points below the 40% target, representing ~$66K in margin that could be recovered through rate increases or contractor cost negotiation.
3.  **Sales Overcapacity (~$80,000 Leakage):** Sales at 8.8% of revenue vs. BiC target of ~5-6% represents ~$145K in excess capacity. If even $80K of that were eliminated or converted to productive pipeline generation, it would flow directly to profit.
4.  **G&A Overhead (~$132,000 Leakage):** G&A at 17.3% vs. a target of ~14-15% represents excess overhead. The senior leadership time allocation (Froio 45%, Walsh 35% to G&A) suggests too much capacity is absorbed in administration vs. billable delivery.

## G. Recommended Actions (Hindsight & Go-Forward)

To close the $507K operating profit gap and align with BiC standards:

1.  **Fix Project Quoting (Immediate):** Require an estimate of internal W-2 hours at fully-loaded cost on every project quote. Never quote a project based solely on subcontractor pass-through costs.
2.  **Enforce 100% Time Entry (Immediate):** Require all scheduled technicians to log 8 hours of time daily in ConnectWise. This is the only way to validate labor allocations.
3.  **Raise Comprehensive Rates (Short-Term):** Negotiate ARC/Bunzl rate increases or reduce contractor costs to achieve 40% margin target.
4.  **Rebalance Sales Capacity (Short-Term):** Mandate self-sourced pipeline quotas. If closing capacity cannot demonstrate ROI, reduce headcount.
5.  **Execute QB Reclassifications (Administrative):** Restructure Chart of Accounts to reflect true COGS vs. SG&A for real-time visibility.
6.  **Separate Product Resale (Administrative):** Track software and hardware resale on a completely separate P&L segment from Managed Services.
