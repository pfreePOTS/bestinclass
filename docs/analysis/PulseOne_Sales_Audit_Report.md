# PulseOne Sales Performance Audit: 2025 vs. 2026

## A. Objective
To evaluate the performance of PulseOne sales representatives across 2025 and early 2026, identify trends in revenue quality and margin generation, and align these findings with Service Leadership Best in Class standards for managed service providers.

## B. Evidence Reviewed
- **2025 Won Opportunities Data:** Detailed record of 772 won deals generating $1.66M in revenue.
- **2026 Won Opportunities Data:** Detailed record of 326 won deals generating $553K in revenue (through June 2026).
- **Sales Review Report (4-21-2026):** Contains 2025 and 2026 monthly sales goals, actuals, and rolling gap analysis.

## C. Current Understanding
PulseOne operates with a mix of recurring agreements, project work, and hardware/software procurement. The company relies on a core group of sales representatives (eanzalone, lwalsh, Ngoodwin, MMoreira, CWiggins) to drive the majority of its revenue. In 2025, the company generated $1.66M in won opportunities at a 35.0% overall margin. In the first half of 2026, the company generated $553K at a 36.2% margin. The overall sales goal for 2026 is $4.8M ($400K per month), indicating a significant gap between current won opportunity pace and overall revenue targets.

## D. Findings

### 1. Overall Revenue and Margin Performance
* **2025 Full Year:** 772 deals, $1,660,307 total revenue, $581,502 margin (35.0% margin). Average deal size: $2,150.
* **2026 Year-to-Date (Through June):** 326 deals, $553,152 total revenue, $200,088 margin (36.2% margin). Average deal size: $1,696.
* **Annualized Pace:** The 2026 annualized pace of $1.1M in won opportunities is approximately 33% below the 2025 full-year total of $1.66M. Furthermore, this pace is drastically below the $4.8M annual revenue goal outlined in the Sales Review Report.

* **The CWiggins Collapse:** CWiggins generated $222K in 2025 but has only closed 8 deals for $10K in the first half of 2026.

### 3. Deal Type and Margin Quality
* **Product vs. Service:** Reps heavily focused on product/procurement (Ngoodwin, MMoreira) consistently yield margins in the 20-30% range. Reps focused on services and projects (lwalsh, CWiggins) yield margins in the 40-50% range.
* **Recurring Revenue:** Agreement wins represent a small fraction of total won opportunities. In 2025, eanzalone drove the bulk of agreement revenue ($185K), largely from Arc Research Institute. In 2026, new agreement wins are minimal ($11K total across all reps).

## E. Service Leadership Best in Class Alignment
* **Below Benchmark - Product Margins:** Service Leadership Best in Class hardware margins typically target 15-20%, while software/cloud resale targets 10-15%. However, blended gross margins for Best in Class MSPs should exceed 45-50%. Reps like Ngoodwin (24% margin) are likely dragging down overall corporate gross margin due to an over-reliance on product renewals rather than high-margin services.
* **Below Benchmark - Recurring Revenue Mix:** Best in Class MSPs target 60-70%+ of total revenue from recurring managed services. The won opportunity data suggests PulseOne's sales team is heavily focused on one-time projects, procurement, and hardware/software renewals rather than net-new managed services agreements.
* **Unclear - Total Revenue vs. Won Opportunities:** The $4.8M 2026 revenue goal in the Sales Review Report vastly exceeds the $1.1M annualized pace of won opportunities. This suggests that either (a) a massive portion of revenue is auto-renewing and not captured in won opportunities, or (b) the sales team is severely underperforming against target.

## F. Risks and Opportunities
* **Risk: Severe Production Drops from Top Reps:** The massive drop-off in production from eanzalone (-75% pace) and CWiggins (-90% pace) represents a critical threat to 2026 revenue goals.
* **Risk: Low Margin Drag:** High-volume reps like Ngoodwin are spending significant time processing low-margin product renewals, which dilutes overall profitability and distracts from selling high-margin services.
* **Opportunity: Replicate lwalsh's Project Success:** lwalsh is successfully selling high-margin (45%), large-dollar projects. Analyzing their sales motion and client targeting could provide a blueprint for other reps.
* **Opportunity: Shift Focus to Managed Services:** The lack of new agreement sales in 2026 is alarming. Incentivizing the sales team to focus on recurring revenue rather than one-time procurement is essential for long-term valuation and stability.

## G. Recommended Actions
1. **Investigate Production Drops:** Immediately review the pipeline and activity levels for eanzalone and CWiggins to determine the root cause of their 2026 production collapse.
2. **Realign Sales Compensation:** Ensure the commission structure heavily favors high-margin services and recurring agreements over low-margin product procurement and renewals.
3. **Offload Administrative Renewals:** Consider moving low-margin, high-volume product renewals (currently handled by Ngoodwin) to an administrative or account management function, freeing up sales reps to hunt for new service revenue.
4. **Conduct a Pipeline Review:** Given the massive gap between the $4.8M revenue goal and the won opportunity pace, conduct a rigorous pipeline review to ensure enough coverage exists to close the gap in the second half of 2026.
