# PulseOne Initial Audit Observations

**A. Objective**
Provide an initial strategic review of PulseOne's financial performance, focusing on revenue quality, gross margin quality, and profitability alignment with the Service Leadership Best in Class framework.

**B. Evidence Reviewed**
The following financial documents were analyzed:
- `Report_from_PulseOne_Group,_LLC 2022 2023 2024.xlsx` (PulseOne Group LLC 3-year P&L)
- `Report_from_PulseOne,_LLC 2022 2023 2024.xlsx` (PulseOne LLC 3-year P&L)
- `2025 Profit and Loss POTS_analysis.xlsx` (2025 Monthly P&L with analytical notes)
- `2025 Balance Sheet POTS_with notes.xlsx` (2025 Balance Sheet)

**C. Current Understanding**
Based on the provided financials, PulseOne operates multiple entities (PulseOne Group LLC and PulseOne LLC). The business mix includes IT Managed Services, Consulting, Hardware/Software Sales, and Agreement Licenses. The 2025 P&L analysis document reveals a total projected or actualized revenue of approximately $5.17M, with IT Managed Services representing about $1.8M and Consulting Income at $1.8M. The business relies heavily on outsourced labor (Upwork, Payoneer) for consulting services delivery.

**D. Findings**
1. **Margin Leakage in Consulting Services:** The 2025 P&L notes indicate that Consulting Services Cost ($1.04M) against Consulting Income ($1.80M) results in a cost ratio of ~57.8%. The internal notes explicitly state this is high and should be <45%, indicating a need to either increase rates or decrease spending.
2. **Software Resale Profitability:** Purchases of Software for Resale ($562K) exceed Sales of Software ($514K) in the 2025 P&L. An internal note flags this: "Is this true? If so, we are losing money here." This requires immediate investigation.
3. **Gross Profit Margin:** The "normalized" Gross Profit calculation in the 2025 analysis shows a Gross Profit of $1.63M on $5.17M revenue, yielding a ~31.6% margin. The notes indicate this represents "18% of revenue leakage, should be 50%".
4. **Partner Compensation:** Partner compensation is recorded at $423K annually in 2025, which is ~8.1% of total revenue.

**E. Service Leadership Best in Class Alignment**
*Note: Provisional alignment based on standard MSP benchmarks, pending upload of specific Service Leadership benchmark definitions to the knowledge base.*
- **Gross Margin:** The normalized gross margin of 31.6% is significantly below the typical Best in Class target of 45-50%+ for a mature MSP.
- **Service Delivery Costs:** The high cost of consulting delivery (>57%) indicates potential inefficiencies in labor utilization or underpricing, misaligned with Best in Class labor efficiency metrics.

**F. Risks and Opportunities**
- **Risk:** The apparent loss on software resale suggests billing omissions, unbilled software licenses, or vendor cost increases not passed to clients.
- **Risk:** High reliance on outsourced consulting labor without adequate margin control threatens overall EBITDA.
- **Opportunity:** Fixing the software billing leakage and optimizing consulting rates/utilization could immediately improve the gross margin closer to the 50% target.

**G. Recommended Actions**
1. **Audit Software Billing:** Immediately reconcile software vendor invoices (e.g., Microsoft, AWS) against client agreements to identify unbilled licenses.
2. **Review Consulting Rates:** Analyze the profitability of consulting engagements to determine if the issue is low billing rates, high outsourced labor costs, or poor utilization.
3. **Upload Benchmark Data:** Upload the specific Service Leadership Best in Class definitions and scorecards to the project for precise comparison.
