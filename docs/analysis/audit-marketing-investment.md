# PulseOne Strategic Audit: Marketing & Sales Investment
**Date:** April 20, 2026  
**Prepared by:** Manus (PulseOne BiC Audit)  
**Status:** Active  

## A. Objective

The objective of this audit is to decompose and evaluate PulseOne's $592K Sales & Marketing (S&M) investment against Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) benchmarks. Specifically, this analysis aims to determine where the marketing budget was deployed, whether it produced measurable pipeline or Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) efficiency, and why Managed Services revenue declined 16.2% year-over-year despite heavy investment. Finally, it assesses the opportunity cost of cold outreach versus expanding the existing Bunzl corporate relationship.

## C. Current Understanding

PulseOne spent approximately $592K on Sales & Marketing in CY 2025, representing 11.5% of total revenue. This figure includes burdened W-2 labor, contractor costs, advertising, marketing tools, and a one-third allocation of partner compensation. Despite this significant investment, which is more than double the BiC target of 5.4%, Managed Services revenue declined 16.2% year-over-year. The S&M function experienced catastrophic turnover in 2025, with five of the seven W-2 marketing and sales staff terminated before year-end. Meanwhile, PulseOne possesses a warm pipeline of 10 uncontracted Bunzl subsidiaries in HubSpot that represent a substantial organic growth opportunity.

## D. Findings

### 1. Marketing Spend Decomposition

The total S&M spend of $591,956 breaks down into four functional categories. The most critical finding is the severe underinvestment in actual demand generation.

| Category | CY 2025 Spend | % of S&M Total | Primary Components |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Brand/Awareness** | $205,764 | 34.8% | General marketing labor ($137K), Advertising & Promotion ($52K), Hootsuite/MailChimp |
| **Sales Enablement** | $172,356 | 29.1% | Direct sales/BizDev labor ($163K), ConnectWise Sell, Calendly |
| **Demand Generation** | $33,276 | 5.6% | HubSpot ($19K), LinkedIn Premium/Ads ($7.7K), Ampl Tagis ($6.3K) |
| **Unclassifiable/Mixed** | $180,560 | 30.5% | Partner Comp allocation ($141K), RepScheduler ($40K) |
| **Total S&M Spend** | **$591,956** | **100.0%** | |

> **Interpretation:** PulseOne spent only $33K (5.6% of the S&M budget) on demand generation tools and campaigns designed to produce measurable leads. The vast majority of the budget was consumed by brand awareness activities that cannot be tied to pipeline, and sales enablement capacity that lacked sufficient lead flow to work. This structural misalignment explains why the high spend failed to produce growth.

### 2. Sales Cost Decomposition and Turnover

The direct sales and business development function cost $172,356, primarily driven by two W-2 roles: Kyle Carey ($57,498 burdened) and Chad Wiggins ($105,450 burdened). However, the broader S&M department suffered a 71% turnover rate in CY 2025. Chad Wiggins, Heather Kohler, Dana Rankin, Anneliese Rivera, and Marcello Rocha Moreira were all terminated. This level of instability destroys campaign continuity, wastes onboarding investment, and makes predictable pipeline generation impossible.

### 3. Pipeline & Conversion Analysis

There is currently no evidence in the uploaded financial or operational files that the $592K S&M investment produced measurable pipeline. Without ConnectWise or HubSpot opportunity data, it is impossible to calculate lead-to-opportunity conversion rates, average deal size, or win rates for new Managed Services agreements. The fact that 277 of PulseOne's 319 clients generate less than $10K annually suggests the sales engine struggles to convert prospects into comprehensive managed services agreements, settling instead for low-value T&M relationships.

### 4. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Estimation

Because the exact number of new Managed Services clients acquired in 2025 is unknown, CAC must be estimated across scenarios. Assuming an average MS agreement value of $75,000/year, a 6-year lifespan, and a 52% gross margin, the Lifetime Value (LTV) of a new client is approximately $234,000.

| Scenario (New MS Clients) | Implied CAC | LTV:CAC Ratio | BiC Health Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 3 new clients | $197,318 | 1.2x | Critical Failure |
| 5 new clients | $118,391 | 2.0x | Below Benchmark |
| 10 new clients | $59,195 | 4.0x | Healthy (>3.0x) |

> **Interpretation:** To justify the $592K S&M spend, PulseOne needed to acquire at least 8 to 10 new, mid-market Managed Services clients in 2025. Given the 16.2% revenue decline, it is highly unlikely this target was met, meaning the CAC was destructively high.

### 5. Revenue Retention vs. Growth Failure

The 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue is likely a combination of contract downgrades and pipeline failure. The Expense Tracker contains a specific note referencing a "Jan 1 2025 - bunzl reduction," indicating that at least one major client reduced scope or seats. Because the S&M engine was structurally misaligned (only 5.6% on demand generation) and destabilized by 71% turnover, it failed to generate the new MS agreements required to offset this existing client contraction.

### 6. Bunzl Expansion Opportunity Cost

PulseOne possesses a warm pipeline of 10 Bunzl subsidiaries in HubSpot that are not yet active in ConnectWise (e.g., Bunzl Agriculture Group, Kishigo, SAS Safety Corp). These entities represent approximately 860 known employees.

Using a conservative estimate of $1,000 in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) per employee for managed services, converting just the top three subsidiaries (Bunzl Agriculture, Kishigo, SAS Safety) would generate **~$685,000 in new ARR**. The estimated cost to execute this expansion (Account Executive time and travel) is roughly $25,000. This represents a 27x ROI ratio. Continuing to spend $239K on cold outreach brand awareness and demand generation while ignoring this warm, high-probability pipeline is a massive strategic opportunity cost.

## F. Risks and Opportunities

The primary risk is **continued value destruction through misaligned marketing spend**. Spending 11.5% of revenue on S&M is only justifiable if it produces top-quartile growth. Spending it on brand awareness while Managed Services revenue shrinks actively destroys EBITDA.

The strategic opportunity is **reallocating capacity to high-probability expansion**. By pausing generic brand marketing and redirecting sales capacity exclusively toward the 10 uncontracted Bunzl subsidiaries and converting the 12 existing pure-T&M clients (e.g., AvePoint) to agreements, PulseOne can generate growth at a fraction of the current CAC.

## G. Recommended Actions

1. **Halt Brand/Awareness Spend:** Immediately freeze the $205K in brand awareness spending (including associated contractor labor and advertising) until a predictable demand generation engine is built.
2. **Launch the Bunzl Expansion Campaign:** Assign the remaining sales capacity (Kyle Carey) exclusively to penetrating the 10 HubSpot Bunzl subsidiaries, leveraging the existing corporate relationship and references from MCR Safety and Bunzl Distribution USA.
3. **Rebalance the S&M Budget:** Restructure the remaining S&M budget to heavily favor demand generation and direct quota-carrying sales, targeting the BiC standard of 5.4% of revenue ($279K).
4. **Implement Pipeline Accountability:** Require a monthly LTV:CAC calculation and pipeline velocity report to justify any future marketing investments.

3. **Sales Pipeline Conversion:** A report from ConnectWise Sell or HubSpot showing the number of MS opportunities created in 2025, the win rate, and the average deal size.
