# PulseOne Strategic Audit: Marketing & Sales Investment
**Date:** April 20, 2026  
**Prepared by:** Manus (PulseOne BiC Audit)  
**Status:** Active (Revised with Owner Confirmations)

## A. Objective

The objective of this audit is to decompose and evaluate PulseOne's $583K Sales & Marketing (S&M) investment against Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) benchmarks. Specifically, this analysis aims to determine where the budget was deployed, evaluate the efficiency of the lead generation engine, and diagnose the structural misalignment between the $52K pipeline-producing spend and the $498K in closing and overhead capacity. It also addresses the impact of the mid-year Bunzl corporate billing consolidation on reported revenue metrics.

## C. Current Understanding

PulseOne spent approximately $583K on Sales & Marketing in CY 2025, representing 11.3% of total revenue. This figure includes burdened W-2 labor, contractor costs, advertising, marketing tools, and a one-third allocation of partner compensation. The S&M investment is more than double the BiC target of 5.4%. Owner confirmations reveal a highly imbalanced model: the entire pipeline was generated by a single $52K advertising line item, while the sales team acted purely as closers with no self-sourced pipeline. Additionally, the apparent 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue is likely a reporting artifact caused by a mid-year consolidation of 10 Bunzl subsidiaries into a single corporate billing entity.

## D. Findings

### 1. Structural Misalignment: The 1:6 Pipeline-to-Capacity Ratio

The total S&M spend of $583,180 breaks down into four functional categories. The most critical finding is the severe imbalance between lead generation investment and closing/overhead capacity.

| Category | CY 2025 Spend | % of S&M Total | Primary Components |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lead Generation (Ad/Promo + Tools)** | $85,246 | 14.6% | Ad & Promo ($52K), HubSpot/LinkedIn/Ampl Tagis ($33K) |
| **Sales / Closing (No Self-Sourced Pipeline)** | $234,388 | 40.2% | Chad Wiggins ($105K), Marcello Moreira ($91K), Kyle Carey (50%, $29K) |
| **Marketing Operations (Brand/Content)** | $82,987 | 14.2% | Kyle Carey (50%, $29K), Heather/Dana/Lucy/Anneliese ($42K) |
| **Unclassifiable / Overhead** | $180,560 | 31.0% | Partner Comp allocation ($141K), RepScheduler ($40K) |
| **Total S&M Spend** | **$583,180** | **100.0%** | |

> **Interpretation:** PulseOne confirmed that the $52K Advertising & Promotion line was the sole source of leads for new clients. The sales team ($234K) generated zero self-sourced pipeline, and the marketing operations team ($83K) produced brand awareness that did not yield measurable leads. For every $1 spent on actual lead generation, PulseOne spent $5.80 on closing capacity and overhead. This structural misalignment means the sales team was starved for pipeline, making their high cost unrecoverable.

### 2. Personnel Turnover and Transitions

The broader S&M department experienced a 71% W-2 termination rate in CY 2025 (5 of 7 W-2 staff terminated). However, owner confirmations clarify that two of these "terminations" (Marcello Moreira and Anneliese Rivera) were actually sequential transitions from W-2 to contractor status. The true departure rate from the S&M function was 43% (Chad Wiggins, Heather Kohler, Dana Rankin). While less catastrophic than initially calculated, this still represents significant instability in the closing and marketing operations functions.

### 3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Estimation

Because the exact number of new Managed Services clients acquired by the $52K ad spend is unknown, CAC must be estimated across scenarios. Assuming an average MS agreement value of $75,000/year, a 6-year lifespan, and a 52% gross margin, the Lifetime Value (LTV) of a new client is approximately $234,000.

| Scenario (New MS Clients) | Implied CAC | LTV:CAC Ratio | BiC Health Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 3 new clients | $194,393 | 1.2x | Critical Failure |
| 5 new clients | $116,636 | 2.0x | Below Benchmark |
| 10 new clients | $58,318 | 4.0x | Healthy (>3.0x) |

> **Interpretation:** To justify the $583K S&M spend, the $52K ad budget needed to generate enough leads for the closers to win at least 8 to 10 new, mid-market Managed Services clients. Without self-sourced pipeline from the $234K sales team, achieving this volume solely through the ad spend is highly improbable, meaning the true CAC was destructively high.

### 4. Revenue Decline Diagnosis: The Bunzl Consolidation

Prior analysis flagged a 16.2% YoY decline in Managed Services revenue and identified a "Bunzl expansion pipeline" in HubSpot. Owner confirmations have completely reversed this diagnosis:
*   All 10 Bunzl subsidiaries in HubSpot are **already under contract**.
*   During a mid-year restructure, billing for these subsidiaries was consolidated through the Bunzl corporate parent (Bunzl Distribution USA).
*   Therefore, the $494,570 revenue attributed to Bunzl Distribution USA likely represents the combined billing of multiple subsidiaries.

> **Interpretation:** The apparent 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue is likely a reporting artifact. When the subsidiaries stopped billing individually, their revenue appeared to "churn" out of the system, while the consolidated corporate billing may not have fully offset the optics of the decline depending on how it was categorized. There is no uncontracted Bunzl expansion pipeline; the focus must shift to ensuring the consolidated contract retained its full value.

## F. Risks and Opportunities

The primary risk is **continued funding of closing capacity without pipeline generation**. Paying $234K for closers who do not self-source leads is only viable if the lead generation engine is producing massive volume. Currently, the lead engine is funded at only $52K.

The strategic opportunity is **rebalancing the S&M ratio**. If the $52K ad spend is truly effective at generating leads, shifting budget from non-lead-producing marketing operations ($83K) into the proven ad spend could saturate the existing closing capacity, dramatically lowering CAC and driving top-line growth.

## G. Recommended Actions

1. **Require Sales Self-Sourcing:** Mandate that the remaining sales capacity (Kyle Carey) dedicate 40% of their time to self-sourcing pipeline via outbound activity, rather than relying entirely on the ad spend.
2. **Reallocate Marketing Ops to Lead Gen:** Shift funding from non-lead-producing brand activities (e.g., Hootsuite, MailChimp, general marketing labor) directly into the $52K Advertising & Promotion engine that is proven to work.
3. **Verify Bunzl Revenue Neutrality:** Conduct a strict audit of the Bunzl mid-year consolidation to ensure the combined corporate billing equals or exceeds the sum of the prior individual subsidiary billings.
4. **Implement LTV:CAC Tracking:** Require a monthly calculation of CAC, explicitly separating the cost of lead generation from the cost of closing capacity.
