## C. 2025 Performance Summary: The True Baseline

PulseOne’s 2025 performance revealed a highly capable core managed services engine whose profitability was obscured by underpriced project delivery, an imbalanced sales/marketing model, and unmanaged software resale pass-throughs.

**The Corrected 2025 Financial Reality:**
*   **Total Revenue:** $5,168,754
*   **Blended Gross Margin:** 37.6% (BiC Target: 44.0%+)
*   **Total SG&A:** 31.6% (BiC Target: 27.4%)
*   **Normalized EBITDA:** 6.3% or ~$326K (BiC Target: 17.5% or ~$904K)

The 11.2-point EBITDA gap represents approximately **$578,000 in missing annual profit** [3]. 

### 1. Successes (Where PulseOne Exceeds BiC)

*   **Managed Services (Help Desk) Efficiency:** The core MSP delivery engine generated $1.8M in revenue at a **73.1% direct delivery gross margin**, far exceeding the BiC target of 48.7–52.4% [4]. The Help Desk team operates at a Service Multiple of Labor of 5.41x (nearly double the BiC target of 2.80x), proving the fundamental service model is highly efficient.
*   **Comprehensive Services (ARC):** The $1.2M ARC staff augmentation contract operated at a healthy **46.1% gross margin** [4]. This contractor-driven model is structurally sound and highly profitable when priced correctly.
*   **Recurring Revenue Strength:** PulseOne maintains a strong recurring revenue base (73.8% of total), providing a stable foundation for the business.

### 2. Failures (Where Profit Leaked)

*   **Project Margin Collapse (~$235K+ Leakage):** The True Projects segment operated at a severe loss. While out-of-pocket subcontractor costs showed a 44.0% margin, the "invisible subsidy" of unbilled internal W-2 labor (Calkins, Froio, Walsh, Harris) dragged the fully-loaded margin down to ~24% or worse [4]. Projects were consistently underpriced because internal labor was not fully burdened in the quotes.
*   **Sales & Marketing ROI Failure (~$80K+ Leakage):** PulseOne spent $358K (6.9% of revenue) on Sales & Marketing, heavily funding closing capacity ($234K) that generated 0% self-sourced pipeline [5]. The entire lead generation engine relied on a single $52K advertising line item. This 1:6 ratio of lead-gen to closing capacity resulted in an unsustainable Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).
*   **Software Pass-Through Margins:** While M365 resale is healthy (~43.4%), the $133K Adobe Creative Cloud pass-through for ARC operated at a near-zero 4.3% margin, destroying blended product profitability [6].
*   **SaaS Tool Sprawl (~$21K Leakage):** The business carried redundant or unnecessary internal SaaS tools (e.g., overlapping scheduling and IDE tools) that inflated G&A overhead.

## D. 2026 Actionable Planning Priorities

To close the $578K profit gap and drive toward the 17.5% BiC EBITDA target, PulseOne must execute the following prioritized initiatives in 2026.

### Priority 1: Fix Project Profitability (Immediate)

The invisible W-2 subsidy is the largest single drain on the company's profitability.

1.  **Mandate Fully-Loaded Quoting:** Effective immediately, all project quotes must include an estimate of internal W-2 hours at fully-loaded cost (salary + payroll taxes/benefits + overhead burden). Never quote a project based solely on subcontractor pass-through costs.
2.  **Implement Margin Gates:** Require partner approval for any fixed-fee project quoted below a 50% fully-loaded gross margin.
3.  **Resolve the QuickBase vs. QuickBooks Discrepancy:** Pull ConnectWise invoices tagged to project boards for CY2025 to determine whether true project revenue was $432K (as mapped in QuickBooks) or $691K (as tracked in QuickBase) [7]. This will dictate whether the segment needs a pricing overhaul or an accounting reclassification.

### Priority 2: Rebalance Sales & Marketing (Short-Term)

The S&M department must shift from a closer-heavy model to a pipeline-generation model.

1.  **Require Sales Self-Sourcing:** Mandate that remaining sales capacity dedicate at least 40% of their time to self-sourcing pipeline via outbound activity. BiC standard requires sales to self-source 30–50% of their pipeline [5].
2.  **Reallocate Marketing Budget:** Shift funding from non-lead-producing marketing operations directly into the proven $52K Advertising & Promotion engine.
3.  **Track LTV:CAC:** Implement a monthly calculation of Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), explicitly separating the cost of lead generation from the cost of closing capacity.

### Priority 3: Optimize Comprehensive & Product Margins (Short-Term)

1.  **Target Bunzl Staffing Pricing:** Ensure all net-new Bunzl staffing proposals are priced to achieve a minimum 40% gross margin against the Blue Pisces (or equivalent) contractor costs [7].
2.  **Attach Procurement Fees:** Immediately review the Adobe Creative Cloud renewal for ARC and attach a standard procurement/management fee to elevate the margin from 4.3% to the BiC 24% floor [6]. Apply this rule to all large software pass-throughs.

### Priority 4: Enforce Operational Discipline & Visibility (Ongoing)

1.  **Mandate 100% Time Entry:** Require all scheduled technicians (including W-2 staff working on projects) to log 8 hours of time daily in ConnectWise. This is the only way to validate labor allocations and eliminate the "invisible subsidy" permanently.
2.  **Execute Chart of Accounts Reclassifications:** Formally move the $194K in service delivery tools (ConnectWise, Flexis, Kaseya) and the service delivery W-2 labor into COGS accounts in QuickBooks to ensure real-time visibility into true gross margin [4].
3.  **Cancel Redundant SaaS:** Execute the cancellation of the ~$21K in identified redundant internal SaaS tools to immediately reduce G&A overhead.
