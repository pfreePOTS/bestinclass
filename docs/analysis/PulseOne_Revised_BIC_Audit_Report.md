# PulseOne Strategic Audit: Revised Service Leadership Best in Class Analysis

**Objective:**
Re-evaluate PulseOne’s financial performance against the Service Leadership Best in Class (BIC) framework, incorporating newly provided partner compensation allocations (2/3 Operations, 1/3 Marketing) and detailed labor classification from the 2023-2025 Expenses Tracking Master.

**Evidence Reviewed:**
- Group LLC and PulseOne LLC historical P&Ls (2022–2024)
- 2025 POTS P&L and Balance Sheet
- `ExpensesTrackingMASTER2023.xlsx` (detailed labor classification and burden rates)
- `PayoneerReportingSheet52322.xlsx` (offshore contractor costs)
- Publicly available Service Leadership BIC benchmark definitions and metrics [1]

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Revised Normalization Impact

The incorporation of the detailed expenses tracking and proper partner compensation allocation significantly changes the *visibility* of PulseOne's costs, though the bottom-line EBITDA remains identical. 

Previously, PulseOne's P&L showed massive SG&A overhead (92.8% of Gross Margin) and very high Gross Margins (58.9%). The revised analysis correctly allocates delivery labor (Help Desk, Services, and 2/3 of partner compensation) to Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). 

**The result is a fundamentally different picture of the business:**
The core issue is not bloated administrative overhead. The core issue is that **service delivery is drastically underpriced or overstaffed**, destroying gross margin before it ever reaches the SG&A line.

## 2. Revised Findings by Service Line

With delivery labor correctly allocated to the service lines that generate the revenue, we can accurately assess the true profitability of each business unit.

### Finding 1: Managed Services is Highly Profitable (Above BIC Target)
When allocating Help Desk labor and 50% of the Operations partner compensation to Managed Services, the unit generates a **48.7% Gross Margin** on $2.56M in revenue. This is an excellent result that sits squarely within the Service Leadership BIC target range of 48-52%. The Help Desk team (both W2 and offshore/Payoneer) is operating efficiently relative to the revenue it supports.

### Finding 2: Consulting Services is Bleeding Cash (Critical Risk)
When allocating Services/Projects labor, subcontractor costs (Basim, David, Blue Pisces), and the remaining 50% of Operations partner compensation to Consulting, the unit generates a disastrous **7.8% Gross Margin** on $1.8M in revenue. 
- The BIC target is 48-52%. 
- PulseOne is paying $1.66M to deliver $1.80M in consulting revenue. 
- This indicates severe underpricing of projects, massive scope creep, or unbilled utilization of highly paid contractors.

### Finding 3: Software Resale is Losing Money
As noted in the initial analysis, PulseOne is generating a **negative 9.4% gross margin** on $514K of software sales. PulseOne is paying vendors $562K for software it only sells for $514K, destroying $48K in pure profit.

## 3. SG&A and Overhead Efficiency

With delivery labor correctly moved to COGS, the SG&A picture improves dramatically. 

### Finding 4: Sales & Marketing Spend is High but Justifiable if Growth Follows
PulseOne spends **9.6% of revenue** ($497K) on Sales & Marketing, which includes the CMO's partner compensation allocation, marketing staff (Daniyal, Kyle, Marcello), and advertising. The BIC target is 5.4%. While higher than benchmark, over-investing in Sales & Marketing is a valid strategic choice *if* it drives high growth in Managed Services. However, Managed Services revenue declined 16.2% year-over-year.

### Finding 5: General & Administrative Overhead is Highly Efficient
PulseOne spends only **14.7% of revenue** ($760K) on G&A, which is significantly better than the BIC target of 22.6%. The administrative team and facility costs are lean and well-managed.

## 4. Recommended Actions

1. **Immediate Consulting Pricing Audit:** The 7.8% gross margin in consulting is the single biggest destroyer of value in the business. You must immediately audit the billing rates and utilization of the primary subcontractors (Basim Mashni, David Fredrick, Blue Pisces/Ruben). Are their hours being fully billed to clients at a 2.5x markup?
2. **Halt Software Losses:** Identify which software products are being sold below cost. This is likely an un-updated pricing table in ConnectWise where vendor costs increased but client prices did not.
3. **Re-evaluate Marketing ROI:** The $497K spent on Sales & Marketing is not producing the required growth in the core Managed Services business. Evaluate the ROI of the offshore BizDev and Marketing contractors.
