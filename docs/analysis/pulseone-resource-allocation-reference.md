## A. Objective
This document serves as the **single source of truth** for how every PulseOne labor resource (W-2, Upwork, and contractor) is allocated across service segments, cost categories, and entities for CY 2025. All segment-level P&L calculations, COGS classifications, and margin analyses must reference this document. It supersedes all prior resource classification assumptions in other analysis documents.

- CY 2025 Payroll Detail Summary (Gross Earnings)
- CY 2025 Upwork Time by Freelancer Report

## C. Current Understanding
PulseOne operates with a blended workforce of W-2 employees, Upwork freelancers, and direct 1099/B2B contractors. Prior analyses relied on assumptions for segment allocation (e.g., classifying all help desk staff as 100% Managed Services COGS). This document applies owner-confirmed allocations, separating Comprehensive Services (ARC staff augmentation) from discrete Consulting/Projects, and removing separate entity costs (RepScheduler and PulseOne Communications) from the operating baseline.

## E. Service Leadership Best in Class Alignment
The isolation of ARC (Comprehensive Services) from Managed Services and Projects is required by BiC methodology. The exclusion of non-operating entity costs (RS/POC) is required for accurate EBITDA calculation. The compensation of partners at FMV means no add-back is appropriate under BiC rules.

## F. Risks and Opportunities
- **Risk:** Without exact project costing time-entry data, the exact margin split between MS and CP remains an estimate. The "invisible subsidy" of W-2 labor continues to distort project profitability.
- **Opportunity:** Clearly separating ARC from the Help Desk shows the core MS delivery engine is highly efficient, allowing management to focus solely on fixing project margins and rationalizing S&M spend.
