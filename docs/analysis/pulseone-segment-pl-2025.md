## G. Risks and Opportunities

**Risk: The Invisible Subsidy ($322K/year, ~$27K/month).** PulseOne's W-2 technicians are spending thousands of hours on project work that clients are never billed for. This artificially inflates the apparent profitability of projects in QuickBase (51.6% vs. true 24.2%) while silently draining capacity from the Managed Services pool. Without ConnectWise time-entry validation, the exact magnitude remains an estimate, but the structural problem is clear.

**Risk: Product Resale Margin Drag ($1.08M COGS on MS).** The $1.08M in product/license COGS (Agreement Licenses $367K, Software for Resale $563K, Other Hardware $149K) collapses the MS margin from 76.6% to 36.6%. This is not a service delivery problem — it is a pricing and packaging problem. The software resale audit documented that PulseOne is reselling software at or below cost.

**Opportunity: Two Surgical Fixes.** These two problems account for nearly all of PulseOne's margin gap to BiC. Fixing project quoting (to include W-2 labor cost) and software resale pricing (to achieve positive margin) would lift the blended company margin from 36.6% toward the 48%+ BiC target without requiring any changes to the service delivery model, which is already performing well above benchmark.

## H. Recommended Actions

1. **Require W-2 Labor Estimates in All Project Quotes.** Every project quote must include an estimate of internal W-2 hours at fully-loaded cost. This is the only way to eliminate the invisible subsidy.
2. **Execute Software Resale Pricing Correction.** Implement the pricing changes identified in the Software Resale Audit to achieve positive margin on the $563K software-for-resale line.
3. **Validate the Invisible Subsidy with ConnectWise Time Data.** Pull CY 2025 time entries for Stephen Calkins, Laura Walsh, James Froio, and Hagen McDonell, segmented by board (Help Desk vs. Projects). This is the single most valuable data pull remaining in the audit.
