## C. Current Understanding of PulseOne

PulseOne is a managed service provider and technology integration company with three primary revenue streams: **IT Managed Services** (recurring agreements), **Consulting/Professional Services** (T&M and project-based), and **Product Sales** (hardware and software resale). The business operates across multiple legal entities (PulseOne Group LLC, PulseOne LLC Delaware) and employs a hybrid labor model combining 27 W-2 employees with a meaningful offshore contractor workforce via Upwork (~$300K/year in 2025).

Total 2025 revenue was **$5.17M**, generating a gross profit of **$3.05M (58.9% GM)** and net income of **$217,775 (4.2% net margin)**. The business has grown modestly from $4.38M in 2022 to $5.17M in 2025, a compound annual growth rate of approximately **5.7%** — well below the BIC growth threshold of 16.3%+.

### D4. Arc Research Institute — Dependency and Margin Profile

Arc Research is PulseOne's largest client by a wide margin at **$1.237M in 2025 (37.2% of total revenue)**. The relationship has several characteristics worth examining closely:

- **Revenue composition:** $1,130,028 in agreement revenue (91.3%) + $119,730 in standard/project work (9.7%) — a very high recurring ratio
- **Monthly agreement value:** Approximately $94,169/month, which includes a large "Comprehensive Services" agreement at ~$95,079/month plus a "Monthly Managed Services" agreement at ~$4,625/month
- **Vendor pass-through:** A significant portion of Arc's COGS is directly tagged in the software COGS file ($114,496 in client-tagged costs, primarily through Ingram Micro), suggesting heavy product/licensing pass-through
- **August spike:** August 2025 shows $95,148 in Standard (project) revenue from Arc — a one-time event that inflated that month's total
- **Risk:** A single contract decision by Arc Research could reduce PulseOne's revenue by over $1.2M. There is no evidence of diversification strategy in the available documents.

### D5. Bunzl Family — Structure and Margin Implications

The Bunzl relationship is structured across multiple independent subsidiaries, which creates a highly favorable risk/reward profile:

- **Bunzl Distribution USA:** $494,570 — mix of agreement ($432,097) and project/standard ($62,473)
- **MCR Safety-Bunzl:** $334,807 — 100% agreement revenue (pure recurring)
- **Bunzl De Mexico:** $78,413 — 97.7% agreement
- **Majestic Glove-Bunzl:** $15,692 — 100% T&M (no agreement)

The Bunzl family is structurally healthier than Arc in terms of revenue quality (higher recurring percentage across entities) and distributed risk.

**Expansion Pipeline:** Beyond the active ConnectWise billing, there are 10 additional Bunzl subsidiaries in HubSpot (including Bunzl Agriculture Group, Liberty Safety, and Intergro) that represent a warm expansion pipeline. This makes Bunzl PulseOne's largest growth opportunity, rather than just a concentration risk.

**The software resale line is operating at a negative gross margin of -9.4%.** The P&L itself contains an analyst note: *"Is this true? If so, we are losing money here."* This is a critical finding. PulseOne is spending $562,726 to generate $514,405 in software revenue — a net loss of $48,320 before any overhead allocation. This likely reflects either (a) cost misclassification, (b) vendor invoices being recorded in a different period than the corresponding revenue, or (c) genuine below-cost software resale.

**Consulting margin is highly variable month-to-month**, ranging from 11.0% (January) to 61.3% (April). This volatility suggests inconsistent labor utilization, project mix variation, or uneven offshore contractor deployment. The annual consulting GM of 42.1% is below the BIC Project/PS benchmark of >24.6% gross margin — but note that BIC's 24.6% is a floor, and the consulting line at 42.1% is actually above that floor. The concern is the **volatility** and the fact that the P&L note flags it as "high" at 57.9% cost ratio (meaning 42.1% GM), suggesting internal targets may be misaligned with BIC definitions.

**Key interpretation:** PulseOne's gross margin profile is actually strong — at 58.9%, it exceeds the BIC blended gross margin benchmark of 44%. The problem is not at the gross margin level. The problem is that **operating expenses consume virtually all of the gross profit**, leaving only 4.2% net margin versus the BIC target of 18.3–19.0%. This is a classic SG&A efficiency problem, not a service delivery margin problem.

The P&L note on advertising and promotion states: *"We are in Best In Class range for expense when you add salaries, including me, but we are underperforming. Should be 15–20% YoY growth."* This is an important self-assessment — management is aware of the growth gap but may be underestimating the SG&A efficiency gap.

## F. Risks and Opportunities

### Critical Risks

**1. Arc Research Concentration (Severity: Critical)**
A single client at 37.2% of revenue creates existential business risk. Any contract renegotiation, loss, or scope reduction at Arc would be immediately devastating to PulseOne's financial position. The August 2025 project spike ($95K) suggests the relationship may be expanding, which is positive, but also deepens dependency.

**2. Bunzl Corporate Dependency (Severity: Moderate)**
While the 27.7% revenue concentration is distributed across independent subsidiaries, PulseOne remains vulnerable to corporate-level IT centralization by Bunzl Group. If the parent company mandates a single national IT provider, PulseOne could lose multiple subsidiaries simultaneously.

**3. Software Resale Negative Margin (Severity: High)**
Losing $48,320 on $514,405 in software revenue is a structural problem. Whether it is a timing issue, a cost classification issue, or genuine below-cost selling, it must be resolved. If it is genuine, PulseOne should either reprice software or exit the resale business.

**4. Revenue Growth Rate (Severity: High)**
At 5.3% YoY growth (2024→2025), PulseOne is growing at one-third the BIC pace. The business is not compounding fast enough to build enterprise value or reach the scale needed for BIC profitability ratios.

**5. SG&A Efficiency Gap (Severity: High)**
Total expenses at 54.7% of revenue versus BIC's 27.4% means PulseOne is spending roughly twice what a BIC firm spends as a percentage of revenue on SG&A. The salary expense line alone ($1,441,010) plus partner compensation ($423,000) totals $1,864,010 — 36.1% of revenue before any other operating expense.

### Opportunities

**1. T&M-to-Agreement Conversion**
$196,835 in T&M-only revenue from engaged clients represents a near-term conversion opportunity. AvePoint ($67,250) and OneOncology ($26,406) are the highest-priority targets.

**2. Long Tail Rationalization**
277 clients generating under $10K each represent a service delivery burden that likely exceeds their contribution margin. A systematic review to identify which of these can be converted to agreements, upsold, or gracefully exited would improve operational efficiency.

**3. Arc Relationship Expansion**
The August 2025 project spike at Arc suggests appetite for additional project work. A structured account growth plan for Arc — while simultaneously reducing dependency through diversification — is the right dual-track strategy.

**3b. Bunzl Subsidiary Expansion**
With 10 additional Bunzl subsidiaries already in HubSpot but not yet billed in ConnectWise, this is PulseOne's most immediate and highest-probability growth vector. A dedicated "land and expand" strategy targeting these specific subsidiaries should be prioritized.

**4. Software Margin Fix**
Resolving the software resale margin issue (whether through repricing, vendor negotiation, or cost reclassification) could recover $48K+ in gross profit immediately.

**5. Offshore Labor Efficiency**
The Upwork model is working for Arc (Matthew Barnett, $83K) and Amiri (Castro, $38K). Expanding this model to other high-volume clients could improve consulting margins without adding W-2 headcount.

## G. Recommended Actions

**Priority 1 — Immediate (0–30 days)**

1. **Reconcile the revenue gap.** The $1.84M difference between ConnectWise-invoiced revenue ($3.33M) and total P&L income ($5.17M) must be explained. Identify which clients, revenue types, or entities are outside ConnectWise. This is foundational to any client-level profitability model.

2. **Investigate the software resale loss.** Pull the software resale detail from QuickBooks and ConnectWise. Determine whether the -9.4% GM is real or a timing/classification artifact. If real, reprice or exit.

3. **Build a formal Arc Research account plan.** Given that Arc is 37.2% of revenue, there should be a documented account plan covering: contract renewal dates, expansion opportunities, relationship health indicators, and contingency planning if the relationship changes.

**Priority 2 — Near-Term (30–90 days)**

4. **Conduct a client profitability ranking.** Use ConnectWise to pull ticket volume, hours logged, and labor cost by client. Combine with the revenue data from the ClientStatement to build a true gross margin by client. This analysis cannot be completed from the available documents alone — it requires ConnectWise operational data.

5. **Identify and pursue T&M-to-agreement conversions.** Target the top 12 T&M-only clients (starting with AvePoint, OneOncology, Frontier Energy) with a structured managed services proposal.

6. **Rationalize the long tail.** Define a minimum viable client threshold (suggested: $5K/year minimum agreement revenue). For clients below this threshold, create a path to either (a) upgrade to a minimum agreement, (b) move to a break-fix/on-demand model with higher T&M rates, or (c) gracefully exit.

**Priority 3 — Strategic (90–180 days)**

7. **Develop a client diversification strategy.** Set a formal policy that no single client should exceed 15% of revenue. This requires a growth plan that adds $2–3M in new revenue from new clients over 24–36 months to dilute the Arc and Bunzl concentration.

8. **Address the SG&A efficiency gap.** The gap between PulseOne's 54.7% SG&A ratio and BIC's 27.4% is the primary reason net margin is 4.2% instead of 18%+. This requires either (a) significant revenue growth without proportional expense growth, or (b) a structured review of the expense base to identify what can be reduced or redeployed.

9. **Formalize a Reference Architecture.** Service Leadership BIC research consistently shows that MSPs with a strict Reference Architecture achieve lower COGS, higher technician utilization, and better margins. The current client base appears highly heterogeneous (319 clients with varied service types). Defining and enforcing a standard technology stack would reduce service delivery complexity and cost.

- **What to pull:** Project-level profitability for all projects closed in 2025
