# PulseOne Sales Mix Breakdown by Representative (Final Revision)
## With Comprehensive Services and IT Assessment Segmentation

### Reclassification Methodology
All deals have been reclassified into the following refined categories based on Opportunity Summary content:

| Category | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Comprehensive Services Agreement** | Large-scale bundled service packages (specifically Arc Research Institute deals) |
| **Managed Services Agreement** | Standard recurring managed services contracts |
| **Managed Services / Install** | ITMS or installation work tied to managed services delivery |
| **IT Assessment (Project)** | IT/Security/Cloud assessments (ideal precursors to MS agreements) |
| **Project** | General one-time project engagements (infrastructure, migrations, consulting) |
| **Software Agreement** | Recurring software subscriptions sold as part of an agreement (Barracuda, M365, etc.) |
| **Software Procurement** | One-time software purchases or license transactions |
| **Software Renewal** | Renewal of existing software subscriptions |
| **Hardware / Other Procurement** | Physical hardware or non-software product sales |
| **Other Renewal** | Non-software renewals (service contracts, warranties, etc.) |

## Key Observations

**1. True Managed Services Agreements are alarmingly low.** 
By separating out the $178K "Comprehensive Services Agreement" for Arc Research Institute and moving IT Assessments to projects, we see that PulseOne's core "Managed Services Agreement" sales were only **$13,990 in 2025** across the entire team. This means the standard recurring revenue sales engine is almost non-existent.

**2. IT Assessments are a healthy pipeline, but are they converting?**
The team sold **$53,462** in IT Assessments in 2025 (led by CWiggins and MMoreira) and **$19,240** in 2026 (led entirely by MMoreira). These assessments carry excellent margins (50-51%). However, if the goal is for these assessments to lead to Managed Services Agreements, the conversion rate appears to be very poor, given the lack of corresponding MS Agreement wins.

**3. eanzalone's performance was heavily dependent on Arc Research.**
eanzalone's 2025 production looked stellar, but 33% of it was tied up in three Comprehensive Services deals for a single client (Arc Research Institute). Their standard MS Agreement sales were only $5,865. In 2026, without Arc Research, their production has fallen sharply.
