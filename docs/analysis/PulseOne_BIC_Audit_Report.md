# PulseOne Strategic Audit: Best-in-Class Alignment

## A. Objective
This report evaluates PulseOne’s historical (2022–2024) and current (2025) financial performance against the Service Leadership Best-in-Class (BIC) framework. The objective is to identify structural inefficiencies, margin leakage, and operational risks that prevent PulseOne from achieving top-quartile profitability, and to provide actionable recommendations for improvement.

## B. Evidence Reviewed
The analysis is based on the following provided documents:
1.  `Report_from_PulseOne_Group,_LLC 2022 2023 2024.xlsx` (Historical P&L)
2.  `2025 Profit and Loss POTS_analysis.xlsx` (Current Year P&L with user notes)
3.  `2025 Balance Sheet POTS_with notes.xlsx` (Current Balance Sheet)
4.  Service Leadership BIC Reference Document (Compiled from public ConnectWise/Service Leadership data)

## C. Current Understanding
PulseOne operates as an IT Solution Provider with approximately $5.17M in annualized revenue (based on 2025 data). The firm has a mixed revenue model, heavily reliant on both IT Managed Services and Consulting. The business is currently experiencing severe profitability and liquidity challenges, with 2025 Adjusted EBITDA estimated at ~4.8%, far below the BIC target of 18–19%.

## D. Findings

### 1. Revenue Quality and Growth
PulseOne’s revenue mix is heavily skewed toward non-recurring sources, which inherently depresses enterprise value and creates revenue volatility.

*   **Recurring Revenue:** In 2025, recurring revenue (IT Managed Services + Agreement Licenses) accounts for only **49.6%** of total revenue. The industry target for a healthy MSP is 70–75%.
*   **Growth Contraction:** While total revenue grew 5.3% year-over-year into 2025, **Managed Services revenue declined by 16.2%**. This indicates a shrinking core business, masked by an increase in lower-margin consulting and software sales.

![Revenue Mix Trend](chart_revenue_mix.png)

### 2. Gross Margin Leakage
While the "Blended Gross Margin" appears high on the P&L (58.9% in 2025), this is an accounting artifact. PulseOne currently records all internal salary expenses in SG&A rather than allocating technical labor to Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). 

*   **Software Resale Losses:** PulseOne is actively losing money on software resale. In 2025, software sales generated $514K in revenue against $562K in purchasing costs, resulting in a **negative 9.4% gross margin**.
*   **Consulting Margin Collapse:** Consulting gross margin (even before internal labor allocation) has degraded significantly, falling from 50.1% in 2022 to **42.1% in 2025** (and as low as 11.9% in 2024). 
*   **Normalized COGS:** When applying Service Leadership normalization rules (estimating that 65% of salary expense belongs in COGS for service delivery), the true Blended Gross Margin drops to approximately **37.1%**, well below the BIC target of 44.0%+.

![Gross Margin Trend](chart_gross_margins.png)

### 3. SG&A Inefficiency and Profitability
PulseOne is severely overspending on overhead relative to the gross margin it produces.

*   **SG&A Efficiency:** In 2025, SG&A expenses consumed **92.8% of all gross margin dollars** generated (before normalization). The BIC target is 58%. This leaves almost nothing to drop to the bottom line.
*   **EBITDA:** Adjusted EBITDA for 2025 is estimated at **4.8%**, compared to the BIC target of 18.3–19.0%+.
*   **Sales & Marketing:** Conversely, PulseOne is *underspending* on growth. Sales & Marketing expenses represent only 1.0% of revenue, compared to the BIC target of 5.4%. However, the firm is highly efficient with the dollars it does spend, generating $58.61 in gross margin for every $1 of S&M spend (BIC target is $8.21).

![EBITDA and SG&A](chart_ebitda_sga.png)

### 4. Liquidity Crisis
The December 31, 2025 Balance Sheet reveals a critical liquidity shortage.

*   **Current Ratio:** PulseOne’s current ratio (Current Assets / Current Liabilities) is **0.10**. The BIC target is 3.00, and anything below 1.0 indicates an inability to meet short-term obligations.
*   **Owner Draws:** The balance sheet shows cumulative owner draws of **$1.83M**, which has severely depleted the firm's equity and cash reserves.

## F. Risks and Opportunities

**Risks:**
1.  **Insolvency:** A current ratio of 0.10 means PulseOne does not have the cash or short-term assets to pay its immediate debts (Accounts Payable of $280K and Credit Cards of $238K).
2.  **Margin Erosion:** The negative margin on software and the declining margin on consulting indicate severe pricing, procurement, or utilization failures.
3.  **Shrinking Core:** The 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue threatens the long-term viability and valuation of the firm.

**Opportunities:**
1.  **Pricing Correction:** Fixing the software resale pricing model will immediately add over $100K to the bottom line without requiring new sales.
2.  **SG&A Rationalization:** If SG&A can be brought down to the 58% of GM$ target, profitability will instantly align with median or BIC peers.
3.  **Chart of Accounts Normalization:** Properly aligning the P&L to the Service Leadership Chart of Accounts will provide management with accurate visibility into true service delivery costs.

## G. Recommended Actions

1.  **Immediate Software Pricing Audit:** Immediately review all software resale agreements. Identify which products are being sold below cost and implement emergency price increases or vendor changes.
2.  **Halt Owner Distributions:** Given the 0.10 current ratio, all non-salary owner draws must be suspended immediately to rebuild cash reserves and pay down high-interest credit card debt.
3.  **Restructure the Chart of Accounts:** Reclassify all technical, service delivery, and dispatch labor from SG&A into COGS. This is a mandatory step for accurate Service Leadership benchmarking.
4.  **Investigate Managed Services Churn:** Conduct a root-cause analysis on the 16.2% decline in Managed Services revenue. Determine if this was due to lost clients, downgraded contracts, or pricing compression.
5.  **Consulting Rate Review:** Evaluate the consulting practice. If subcontractor costs (e.g., Upwork, Payoneer) are consuming 58% of consulting revenue, bill rates must be increased or subcontractor utilization must be optimized.
