# PulseOne Strategic Restructuring Plan: Client Economics & Cost Reduction (V2)

**Date:** May 2026  
**Objective:** Execute a structural cost reduction plan to offset declining revenue and align with Service Leadership Best in Class standards, driven by shedding unprofitable accounts and eliminating the staff capacity those accounts previously required.

---

## 1. The Core Logic: Avoiding the Double-Count

A critical error in previous cost-reduction models is double-counting savings. You cannot claim that "shedding a bad client saves $10,000" AND "firing the technician saves $6,000" for the same unit of work. The $10,000 cost of the client *is* the technician's salary (plus overhead).

**The Corrected Logic Model:**
1. **Shedding clients does not directly save money.** It removes revenue (a negative impact to the P&L) and simultaneously frees up labor hours.
2. **Staff reductions are the actual savings.** The freed labor hours from shed clients provide the operational justification to eliminate staff without degrading service to remaining clients.
3. **Net Improvement = (Total Staff Cost Reductions) - (Revenue Lost from Shed Clients)**

2. **Deepen Contractor Cuts (+$3,480/mo):** Increase Roe Tech reduction to 60% (+$1,660) and My IT reduction to 75% (+$1,820).
3. **W-2 Tech Reductions (+$2,723/mo):** Reduce Goodwin hours by 25% (+$1,572) and McDonell by 20% (+$1,151).
4. **Eliminate Dispatch Role (+$2,600/mo):** Eliminate Alexandria's role and have Laura Walsh absorb dispatch duties within her remaining hours.
5. **Increase Calkins Furlough (+$867/mo):** Move from 15% to 20% reduction.

Executing all of the above options would add **~$11,170/month** in savings, bringing the total net improvement to **~$34,000/month**, requiring either further W-2 terminations or a rebound in Bunzl project revenue to fully close the $40K gap.
