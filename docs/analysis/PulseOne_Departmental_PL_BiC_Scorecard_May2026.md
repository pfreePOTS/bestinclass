## A. Objective
To reconstruct the PulseOne P&L using strict Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) departmental allocations. This model separates operating performance from owner compensation and debt service, revealing the true structural health of the business at the current $325,000/month revenue baseline [1].

## B. The Operating Revelation
When costs are properly allocated to BiC departments, the narrative changes significantly from the raw cash-flow view. PulseOne's core service delivery engine is actually performing at or above Best in Class levels. The entire structural deficit is isolated to a single department: **Sales & Marketing**.

At $325,000/month in revenue, the business generates **$55,041/month in operating profit** (16.9% EBITDA proxy), which is just 1.4 percentage points shy of the BiC target [1]. The perceived "cash burn" occurs entirely below the operating line, where the $55K operating profit is consumed by $35,250 in partner compensation and $20,000 in debt/distributions.

## E. Where Every Dollar Goes (Cash Flow View)

While the operating P&L is healthy, the cash flow math explains the current squeeze. Every $1.00 of the $325,000 in monthly revenue is spent as follows:

1.  **Service Delivery:** $0.41 (Labor $0.28, Tools $0.04, Pass-throughs $0.09)
2.  **Sales & Marketing:** $0.15 (Labor $0.14, Tools $0.01)
3.  **G&A Operating:** $0.20 (Labor $0.14, Overhead $0.06)
4.  **Partner Compensation:** $0.11 ($35,250 total across 3 partners)
5.  **Debt & Distributions:** $0.06 ($20,000 fixed)
6.  **Total Outflow:** $0.93
7.  **Net Cash Margin:** $0.07 ($22,750/month)

*Wait, if the outflow is $0.93, why is there a perceived cash burn?*
Because the $325K revenue target includes $150K+ in Standard/Project revenue. If actual May revenue comes in closer to $275K (just the recurring base + minimal projects), the fixed costs ($49K S&M + $66K G&A + $35K Partner + $20K Debt = $170K) overwhelm the gross margin, creating the burn.

## F. The Path Forward

The BiC model makes the solution mathematically obvious. You do not have a service delivery problem. You have a Sales & Marketing overcapacity problem.

To stabilize the company and protect partner compensation without requiring $400K+ in revenue:

1.  **Enforce the 5.4% S&M Benchmark:** At $325K revenue, your total S&M budget should be **$17,550/month**. You are currently spending $49,609. 
2.  **Immediate Cuts ($12,000/mo):** Eliminate Marcello and Gianpaolo ($5.8K), who are producing zero pipeline. Consolidate HubSpot, LinkedIn, and non-essential tools ($6.2K).
3.  **The 90-Day Clock:** The remaining $37,600/month S&M spend (Hinkley, the 4 new reps, Kyle, Daniyal) must produce $15,000/month in new MRR by August 1 to justify its existence. If it fails, you must reduce the S&M headcount to match the 5.4% benchmark.
