## E. Revenue Ramp Assumptions

### E.1 The Assessment Pipeline (Months 1-4)

The sales process is built around a structured funnel. Every new MS prospect must first undergo a paid technology assessment (averaging $2,000). 
- **Month N:** Assessment performed (generates immediate cash flow)
- **Month N+1:** 50% of assessments convert to Managed Services agreements (MRR begins; onboarding month)
- **Month N+3:** The MS Multiplier kicks in (project work begins flowing from the new client)

During the first 3-4 months, new reps focus heavily on selling assessments. This generates cash flow to offset base salaries and builds the pipeline that will convert to MRR.

### E.2 MRR Conversion & Multiplier Phase (Months 5-8)

As the assessment pipeline matures, the 50% conversion rate begins producing steady MRR additions (averaging $2,000/mo per new client). Two months after each new MS client signs, the **MS Multiplier** begins generating additional project/product revenue at a 1.0x ratio to their MRR.

### E.3 Stabilized Production (Months 7-12)

By month 7, all reps should be in a steady production cadence. The Thrive team's earliest MRR cohorts begin rolling off the 50% commission tier into the 25% and then 10% tiers, dramatically improving net contribution.

## K. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: The 50% Commission Valley.** The Thrive structure means new MRR is essentially break-even from a cash flow perspective during the first 3 months. 
> **Mitigation:** The Internal Team's zero-commission MRR provides a counterbalance. Prioritize Internal Team closing velocity in months 1-4 to build a GP cushion. The 1.0x multiplier effect also helps bridge this valley by generating 10% commission project work alongside the 50% commission MRR.

**Risk 2: Assessment Conversion Failure.** If the product-heavy early ramp fails to convert into MRR by months 4-5, the base salaries become a pure drag on EBITDA.
> **Mitigation:** Implement the structured assessment-to-MRR conversion process (Section I). Monitor conversion rates weekly starting Month 3.

**Risk 3: Service Delivery Quality Degradation.** Rapid new logo acquisition without adequate onboarding discipline can create service desk overload, leading to SLA failures and early churn.
> **Mitigation:** Enforce the capacity triggers in Section J. No new client should be onboarded without a documented 30-day stabilization plan.
