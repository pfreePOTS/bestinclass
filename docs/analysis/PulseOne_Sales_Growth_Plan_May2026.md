# PulseOne Sales Growth Plan (May 2026)

## A. Objective
This document outlines a 12-month structured sales growth plan focused strictly on **New Logo Acquisition**. The objective is to map the path from the post-Phase 1 stabilized baseline (approx. $310,605 monthly revenue / +$23,218 EBITDA) to a robust $5M+ annual run rate and ultimately to Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) profitability targets (15-20% net profit).

## B. Evidence Reviewed
- **Sales Review Report (April 2026):** Validates recent run rates and standard invoicing ($101,526 in April).
- **QuickBase Completed Projects (Jan 2025 - Apr 2026):** Confirms typical deal sizes (median $5,000, average ~$11,891 in 2026) and unburdened project margins (averaging 76% unburdened, normalizing to ~60% target).
- **Thrive Commission Plan:** Details the $2K non-recoverable base + 50%→25%→10%→5% MRR decay structure for new reps.
- **Phase 1 Remediation Plan:** Establishes the stabilized starting baseline and operational capacity.

## C. Current Understanding
- **Baseline Revenue:** The model assumes existing business continues at a conservative baseline of $291,000/month, plus the retained portion of standard/project revenue, bringing the starting point to $310,605/month.
- **Capacity:** Following the Phase 1 cuts and client departures (ARC, Conejo, Sea Ranch), the service desk has adequate capacity to absorb initial new logo growth without immediate headcount additions.
- **Sales Motion:** The early months (1-3) of a new rep's ramp are heavily skewed toward product sales (hardware, software, assessments, Quick Starts) with minimal new MRR. Meaningful MRR conversion typically begins in months 4-5.

## E. Growth Trajectory & Scenarios

Given the ramp time for new reps, the revenue model assumes a "Product-Heavy Early Ramp" transitioning to an "MRR-Driven" model.

### Scenario A: Product-Heavy Early Ramp (Months 1-3)
- **Focus:** Hardware refreshes, software licensing, paid assessments, and Quick Start projects.
- **Deal Profile:** QuickBase data shows median project sizes around $5,000. We anticipate 2-3 of these per rep per month during ramp.
- **Margin Profile:** Projects targeted at 60% GM (unburdened); Software at 30%; Hardware at 25%.
- **Impact:** Generates immediate cash flow to offset the $2K/month base salaries, but does not build the recurring revenue base.

### Scenario B: MRR Conversion Phase (Months 4-6)
- **Focus:** Converting assessments and Quick Starts into Managed Services (MS) agreements.
- **Margin Profile:** MS targeted at 57% GM (Service Leadership BiC target).
- **Commission Impact:** The 50% commission rate for the first 3 months of MRR heavily suppresses net margin initially. A $5,000/mo MRR deal yields only $2,500/mo in net revenue during this period.

### Scenario C: Stabilized Blended Mix (Month 7+)
- **Focus:** Balanced mix of MS (70%), Projects (20%), and Product (10%).
- **Commission Impact:** Commission drops to 25%, then 10%, significantly improving net profitability on the cohorts closed in Months 4-6.

## F. Key Milestones & Targets

### Milestone 1: Restore Furloughs & Establish Cash Buffer
- **Target:** $30,000/month net cash (requires ~$50,000/month EBITDA).
- **Requirement:** Generate approximately $20,000 in new monthly Gross Profit.
- **Path:** Given the 50% initial commission hit on Thrive deals, this milestone is best achieved through the **Internal Team** closing 3-4 average-sized MS deals ($3K-$5K MRR each) at zero commission, combined with project revenue from the Thrive team's early ramp.

### Milestone 2: $5M Annual Run Rate
- **Target:** $416,666/month revenue.
- **Requirement:** Add ~$106,000 in monthly revenue above the Phase 1 baseline.
- **Path:** Achieved around Month 8-9 as the Thrive reps' early cohorts pass the 3-month mark (commission drops from 50% to 25%), and cumulative MRR builds.

### Milestone 3: Service Leadership Best in Class (15-20% Net Profit)
- **Target:** 15-20% Net Profit on a $5M+ run rate.
- **Requirement:** Strict adherence to 57%+ MS Gross Margin and 60%+ Project Gross Margin.
- **Path:** Achieved in Months 10-12+ as commission rates decay to 10% and 5%, maximizing the lifetime value of the acquired MRR.

## G. Risks and Opportunities

- **Risk - The 50% Commission Valley:** The Thrive structure (50% MRR commission for 3 months) means new MRR is essentially break-even or slightly negative from a cash flow perspective initially. The company must rely on the Internal Team and early product/project sales to bridge this gap.
- **Opportunity - Internal Team Leverage:** The non-commissioned Internal Team is the most profitable growth engine. Every dollar of MRR they close drops directly to the bottom line at the standard gross margin rate.
- **Risk - Assessment Conversion:** If the product-heavy early ramp (assessments/Quick Starts) fails to convert into MRR in Months 4-5, the $2K base salaries will become a pure drag on EBITDA.
