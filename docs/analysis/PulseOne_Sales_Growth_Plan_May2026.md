## E. Revenue Ramp Assumptions

The ramp model is calibrated against actual 2026 YTD performance. Marcello has been the only active rep and has not closed significant new MRR, which informs the conservative early-month assumptions for the Thrive team. The Internal Team is expected to produce faster because they are working a warm territory (SoCal) with established PulseOne brand recognition.

### E.1 Product-Heavy Early Phase (Months 1-3)

During the first three months, new reps focus on activities that generate immediate revenue without requiring long sales cycles: paid technology assessments ($2,500-$5,000 each), Quick Start implementations ($5,000-$15,000), hardware refreshes, and software licensing. This approach serves three purposes: it generates cash flow to offset base salaries, it builds client relationships that convert to MRR, and it demonstrates competence to prospects.

QuickBase data confirms this is realistic. In 2026, PulseOne completed 38 projects with the following deal size distribution:

| Deal Size | Count | % of Total |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Under $2,000 | 11 | 29% |
| $2,000 - $5,000 | 9 | 24% |
| $5,000 - $10,000 | 5 | 13% |
| $10,000 - $25,000 | 10 | 26% |
| $50,000 - $100,000 | 3 | 8% |

The median deal size of $5,000 and average of $11,891 support the assumption that each Thrive rep can close $8,000-$12,000/month in product/project revenue during ramp. The average monthly project revenue across Jan-Apr 2026 was $112,964 from the existing team — the new logo plan assumes incremental volume on top of this.

### E.2 MRR Conversion Phase (Months 4-6)

Assessments and Quick Starts performed in months 1-3 begin converting to Managed Services agreements. The Internal Team, working warmer leads, is expected to begin producing MRR earlier (month 1-2) while the Thrive team's first meaningful MRR conversions arrive in months 3-5. This is the critical inflection point — if assessments do not convert, the base salaries become a pure drag.

### E.3 Stabilized Production (Months 7-12)

By month 7, all reps should be in a steady production cadence. The Thrive team's earliest MRR cohorts begin rolling off the 50% commission tier into the 25% and then 10% tiers, dramatically improving the net contribution of each deal. The Internal Team continues adding MRR at zero commission cost.

## J. Assessment-to-MRR Conversion Strategy

The growth plan depends critically on converting early product/assessment engagements into recurring Managed Services agreements. This connects directly to Phase 2 Operational Efficiency Item #7 (Improve Assessment-to-MRR Conversion Rate).

**Current State:** Assessments and Quick Starts are being sold but conversion to full MS agreements is inconsistent. There is no standardized handoff process from the assessment deliverable to a proposal for ongoing services.

**Required Process:**

1. Every paid assessment must include a "Current State vs. Recommended State" deliverable that naturally leads to an MS proposal.
2. The assessment SOW must include language scheduling a "findings presentation" meeting where the MS proposal is presented alongside the assessment results.
3. Walsh/Batsford/Calkins must be present at the findings meeting to close — the Thrive rep who sold the assessment should not be solely responsible for the MS close.
4. Target conversion rate: 60% of assessments should convert to MS agreements within 90 days of assessment completion.
5. Track conversion rate by rep monthly. Reps consistently below 40% conversion need coaching intervention.

## L. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: The 50% Commission Valley.** The Thrive structure means new MRR is essentially break-even from a cash flow perspective during the first 3 months of each deal. If all four reps begin producing MRR simultaneously in Month 4-5, the commission expense spike could temporarily suppress EBITDA growth.

> **Mitigation:** The Internal Team's zero-commission MRR provides a counterbalance. Prioritize Internal Team closing velocity in months 1-4 to build a GP cushion before the Thrive commission spike hits.

**Risk 2: Assessment Conversion Failure.** If the product-heavy early ramp (assessments, Quick Starts) fails to convert into MRR by months 4-5, the $8,000/month in Thrive base salaries plus $4,507 for Marcello becomes a pure drag on EBITDA ($12,507/month or $150K annualized).

> **Mitigation:** Implement the structured assessment-to-MRR conversion process (Section J). Monitor conversion rates weekly starting Month 3. If conversion is below 30% by Month 4, intervene with additional training or territory reassignment.

**Risk 3: SoCal Territory Slower Than Expected.** Harris and Goodwin are working Chad Wiggins' former territory. If that territory has been neglected or relationships have cooled, the Internal Team's pipeline may take longer to build than projected.

> **Mitigation:** Harris and Goodwin should prioritize re-engaging Wiggins' former prospects and any dormant leads in the first 30 days. If pipeline is thin by Day 45, expand territory scope or redirect to inbound lead follow-up.

**Risk 4: Service Delivery Quality Degradation.** Rapid new logo acquisition without adequate onboarding discipline can create service desk overload, leading to SLA failures and early churn.

> **Mitigation:** Enforce the capacity triggers in Section K. No new client should be onboarded without a documented 30-day stabilization plan. Track first-90-day ticket volume per new client against capacity model.
