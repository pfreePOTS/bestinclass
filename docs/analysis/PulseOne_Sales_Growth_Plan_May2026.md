## A. Objective

This document presents a structured 12-month sales growth plan focused exclusively on **new logo acquisition**. The existing book of business is assumed to continue at current run rates; this plan models only the incremental revenue, gross profit, commission costs, and net contribution from new clients won during the planning period. **The MS Multiplier Effect:**
A critical Service Leadership Best in Class (BiC) concept is the "multiplier effect" of Managed Services. New clients require onboarding projects, hardware refreshes, and software licensing. Based on PulseOne's actual performance ratio (~$150K MS to ~$130K Standard), this model assumes a **1.0x portfolio-level multiplier**. For every $10,000 in new MRR added, the portfolio generates an expected $10,000 in ongoing project/product revenue. This is modeled with a conservative 2-month lag (MRR signed in Month 1 begins generating multiplier revenue in Month 3).

## E. Revenue Ramp Assumptions

### E.1 Product-Heavy Early Phase (Months 1-3)

During the first three months, new reps focus on activities that generate immediate revenue without requiring long sales cycles: paid technology assessments ($2,500-$5,000 each) and Quick Start implementations ($5,000-$15,000). This generates cash flow to offset base salaries and builds client relationships that convert to MRR. QuickBase data confirms median deal sizes around $5,000.

### E.2 MRR Conversion Phase (Months 4-6)

Assessments and Quick Starts performed in months 1-3 begin converting to Managed Services agreements. The Internal Team, working warmer leads, begins producing MRR earlier (month 1-2) while the Thrive team's first meaningful MRR conversions arrive in months 3-5. As MRR builds, the **MS Multiplier** begins generating additional project/product revenue.

### E.3 Stabilized Production (Months 7-12)

By month 7, all reps should be in a steady production cadence. The Thrive team's earliest MRR cohorts begin rolling off the 50% commission tier into the 25% and then 10% tiers, dramatically improving net contribution.

## I. Assessment-to-MRR Conversion Strategy

The growth plan depends critically on converting early product/assessment engagements into recurring Managed Services agreements. This connects directly to Phase 2 Operational Efficiency Item #7 (Improve Assessment-to-MRR Conversion Rate).

**Current State:** Assessments and Quick Starts are being sold but conversion to full MS agreements is inconsistent.

**Required Process:**
1. Every paid assessment must include a "Current State vs. Recommended State" deliverable that naturally leads to an MS proposal.
2. The assessment SOW must include language scheduling a "findings presentation" meeting where the MS proposal is presented alongside the assessment results.
3. Walsh/Batsford/Calkins must be present at the findings meeting to close — the Thrive rep who sold the assessment should not be solely responsible for the MS close.
4. Target conversion rate: 60% of assessments should convert to MS agreements within 90 days of assessment completion.

## K. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: The 50% Commission Valley.** The Thrive structure means new MRR is essentially break-even from a cash flow perspective during the first 3 months. 
> **Mitigation:** The Internal Team's zero-commission MRR provides a counterbalance. Prioritize Internal Team closing velocity in months 1-4 to build a GP cushion. The 1.0x multiplier effect also helps bridge this valley by generating 10% commission project work alongside the 50% commission MRR.

**Risk 2: Assessment Conversion Failure.** If the product-heavy early ramp fails to convert into MRR by months 4-5, the base salaries become a pure drag on EBITDA.
> **Mitigation:** Implement the structured assessment-to-MRR conversion process (Section I). Monitor conversion rates weekly starting Month 3.

**Risk 3: Service Delivery Quality Degradation.** Rapid new logo acquisition without adequate onboarding discipline can create service desk overload, leading to SLA failures and early churn.
> **Mitigation:** Enforce the capacity triggers in Section J. No new client should be onboarded without a documented 30-day stabilization plan.
