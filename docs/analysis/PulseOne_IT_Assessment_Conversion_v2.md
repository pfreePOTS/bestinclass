# PulseOne IT Assessment Conversion Analysis (Corrected)

## Evaluating the Bridge from Project to Managed Services

### A. Objective

To determine the conversion rate of IT Assessments (sold as one-time projects) into recurring Managed Services Agreements. This analysis traces every client who purchased an IT Assessment in 2025 or 2026 to see if they subsequently signed a Managed Services contract.

*Note: This report has been updated to strictly separate true Managed Services delivery from hardware procurement (e.g., laptops, shipping) that was previously miscategorized under "ITMS/Install" in the source data.*

### B. Evidence Reviewed

- **2025 & 2026 Won Opportunities Data**

- **Methodology:** A client is considered "Converted to MS" if they purchased an IT Assessment and subsequently won an opportunity categorized as a "Managed Services Agreement", "Managed Services / Install" (excluding hardware purchases), or "Comprehensive Services Agreement".

### C. Executive Summary

Across 2025 and 2026, PulseOne sold **28 unique IT Assessments** generating **$72,702** in project revenue.

The overall conversion rate from IT Assessment to Managed Services client is **46%** (13 of 28 clients).

However, the actual *Managed Services revenue yield* is extremely low. The 13 converted clients generated only **$20,723** in subsequent true Managed Services revenue—meaning the assessments themselves ($72K) generated more than 3x the revenue of the resulting managed services contracts.

### G. Service Leadership Best in Class Implications & Recommendations

**1. The Assessment-to-MRR Engine is Broken**In a Best in Class MSP, paid IT Assessments are the primary vehicle for acquiring new Managed Services Agreements (MRR). While PulseOne is successfully selling the assessments (28 deals), the resulting MS contracts are almost non-existent. Generating only $20K in true MS revenue from 13 "converted" assessments means the sales team is closing tiny, fractional service agreements (or just labor for Windows 11 refreshes) rather than comprehensive, all-in managed services contracts.

**2. Assessments are Leading to Hardware and Projects, Not Agreements**
When we look at clients like Pelage Pharmaceuticals ($23K in hardware/projects vs $425 in MS), it is clear the assessments successfully uncover needs. However, the sales team is selling those solutions as one-time Projects or Hardware transactions rather than wrapping them into a long-term Managed Services Agreement. For the majority of unconverted assessments, there is zero follow-on business at all.

**3. Arc Research Institute Impact**It should be noted that PulseOne's largest "Comprehensive Services" client, Arc Research Institute (which accounted for $178K in 2025 revenue), is no longer a client as of April 1, 2026. This massive loss of recurring revenue makes fixing the Assessment-to-MRR conversion engine an existential priority.

**Recommended Action:**PulseOne must completely overhaul its post-assessment presentation process. Sales reps should be required to present a comprehensive Managed Services Agreement as the primary solution to the risks uncovered in the assessment, rather than allowing clients to cherry-pick one-time hardware purchases or project fixes.
