## A. Objective
This document presents the definitive, BiC-aligned Departmental Profit & Loss statement for PulseOne for CY 2025. It serves as the master financial model for the business, translating raw QuickBooks line items into the four BiC service segments (Managed Services, Comprehensive Services, True Projects, and Products) while properly allocating labor burden, SG&A overhead, and non-operating exclusions.

This P&L achieves **$0 variance** to the QuickBooks Net Income of $217,774, ensuring absolute financial integrity while exposing the true operational margins of each business line.

## C. Methodology & Key Reclassifications
To achieve BiC alignment, the following structural adjustments were made to the raw QuickBooks data:

1. **Labor Allocation:** All W-2, Upwork, and offshore labor was distributed across segments based on the exact percentages in the Resource Allocation Reference v4.0. The $204K negative variance between the theoretical W-2 model and actual QB Salary Expense was absorbed proportionally across all labor lines to ensure exact reconciliation.
2. **Cost of Delivery (COD):** The project management and coordination labor (Kaitlin Harris, Laura Walsh) was split 90% to True Projects and 10% to Comprehensive Services.
3. **Software Reclassification:** The $366,527 "Agreement License Cost" (M365, Barracuda, etc. bundled into MS agreements) was moved from Product COGS to Managed Services COGS. Skout ($72,960) and EFolder ($10,719) were reclassified from Product COGS to MS Tools.
4. **Subcontractor Reclassification:** The $1.04M "Consulting Services-Cost" was split between Comprehensive Services (ARC + Bunzl staffing), True Projects, and Managed Services based on vendor identity.
5. **Non-Operating Exclusions:** RepScheduler development/platform costs ($117K) and PulseOne Communications staff ($33K) were moved below the line to calculate Normalized EBITDA.

## E. Findings & Best in Class Alignment

### 1. Managed Services (56.2% GM) — *Above Benchmark*
The core MS engine is highly profitable. By isolating the true cost of delivery (W-2 help desk, Upwork, offshore, and internal tools), the MS segment operates at 56.2% gross margin, exceeding the BiC target of 48-52%. This indicates strong pricing discipline and an efficient, heavily outsourced Level 1/2 labor model.

### 2. Comprehensive Services (41.0% GM) — *Aligned*
The staffing augmentation model (ARC and Bunzl Blue Pisces) operates at a clean 41.0% gross margin. This perfectly aligns with the BiC target of ~40% for staffing/comprehensive services. The 10% COD overlay accurately captures the management burden of maintaining these contractor relationships.

### 3. True Projects (6.0% GM) — *Critical Failure*
True Projects generated $434K in revenue but cost $408K to deliver, resulting in a near-zero margin (6.0%). The BiC target is 40%.
- **Root Cause:** The segment suffers from an "invisible subsidy" of highly paid W-2 engineering labor (Calkins, Froio, Hagen) and heavy Cost of Delivery overhead (Harris, Walsh). 
- **Implication:** PulseOne is effectively giving away complex engineering and project management for free, charging only enough to cover the external subcontractor costs (Vincent, Matt, Roe Tech).

### 4. Products (-17.6% GM) — *Critical Failure*
The Products segment generated $522K in revenue but incurred $614K in COGS, resulting in a structural loss of $92K.
- **Root Cause:** Even after correcting the AvePoint margin assumption (to 29.8%), the massive gap between Ingram Micro/Synnex purchasing costs and recognizable product revenue remains.
- **Implication:** The company is losing money on hardware/software resale. This requires an immediate audit of quoting templates, markup rules, and vendor rebate capture.
