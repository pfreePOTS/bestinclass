## A. Objective

To present the definitive CY 2025 Profit & Loss statement for PulseOne, mapped to the four Service Leadership business models (Managed Services, Comprehensive Services, True Projects, Products). 

**Version 5.0 Update:** This version represents a structural shift from prior models. Instead of using annualized estimates or scaling factors, this model uses a **strict top-down allocation** where every single dollar from the QuickBooks P&L is allocated exactly once. It uses actual gross pay from the ADP payroll report and actual contractor payments from the Upwork and Payoneer reporting sheets. It reconciles to the QuickBooks Net Income ($217,774) with $0 variance.

## B. Evidence Reviewed

- CY 2025 Payroll Detail Summary (Gross Earnings from ADP)
- Payoneer Reporting Sheet (CY 2025 actuals)

- Consulting Services - Cost detail report (`2025PulseOneConsultingServicesCost.xlsx`)

## C. Methodology & Key Constraints

1. **Total Cost Constraint:** The total costs in this model ($4,950,980) match the QuickBooks total costs exactly. No costs were invented, and no costs were dropped.
2. **Actual Labor Costs:** W-2 labor uses actual gross pay from the COGS Validation audit. Offshore labor uses actual 2025 payments from Payoneer ($172,842) and Upwork ($299,924).
3. **Payroll Burden:** Payroll taxes ($296,256) and payroll expenses ($14,620) are allocated proportionally across the P&L based on the destination of the W-2 labor that generated them.
4. **Salary Expense Gap:** The model identifies exactly who is paid through the QB "Salary Expense" line. The sum of those individuals is $57,592 *less* than the QB line total. This $57K of unidentified labor cost is held in SG&A (G&A Other) to ensure mathematical reconciliation without distorting segment gross margins.

## G. Findings & Best in Class Alignment

### 1. Managed Services (52.5% GM) — *Above Benchmark*
The core MS engine is highly profitable. By isolating the true cost of delivery (W-2 help desk, Upwork, Payoneer, internal tools, and 10% of onboarding/offboarding project management), the MS segment operates at 52.5% gross margin, exceeding the BiC target of 48-52%. This is the economic engine of PulseOne.

### 2. Comprehensive Services (40.3% GM) — *Aligned*
The staffing augmentation model (ARC and Bunzl Blue Pisces) operates at a clean 40.3% gross margin. This perfectly aligns with the BiC target of ~40% for staffing/comprehensive services. The model includes the actual BILL payments ($881K) plus the 10% COD overlay to capture management burden.

### 3. True Projects (-3.8% GM) — *Critical Failure*
True Projects generated $434K in revenue but cost $451K to deliver, resulting in a net loss at the gross margin line (-3.8%). The BiC target is 40%.
- **Root Cause:** The segment suffers from an "invisible subsidy" of highly paid W-2 engineering labor (Calkins, Froio, Hagen), heavy Cost of Delivery overhead (Harris, Walsh), and the associated payroll tax burden. 
- **Implication:** PulseOne is paying its engineers to do project work, but only charging the client enough to cover the external subcontractor costs (Vincent, Barnett, Roe Tech).

### 4. Products (-17.6% GM) — *Critical Failure*
Hardware and software resale generated $522K in revenue but cost $614K, resulting in a severe loss.
- **Root Cause:** As identified in the Software Resale audit, the cost of goods sold from Ingram Micro and Synnex vastly exceeds the revenue collected for those products.
- **Implication:** PulseOne is subsidizing client hardware and software purchases out of its Managed Services profits.

## H. Strategic Conclusion

PulseOne's 6.4% EBITDA is entirely the result of the highly efficient Managed Services segment (52.5% GM) subsidizing two broken segments: Projects (-3.8% GM) and Products (-17.6% GM). 

If PulseOne could bring Projects to a standard 30% GM and Products to mere breakeven (0% GM), **EBITDA would increase by $238,000**, jumping from 6.4% to **11.0%** — immediately placing the company within striking distance of Best in Class performance.
