# PulseOne Strategic Audit: 2025 Profit Leakage Waterfall (Corrected)

## A. Objective
The objective of this audit is to provide a forensic "where did the money go" analysis for CY 2025. By mapping PulseOne's actual expenses against Service Leadership Best-in-Class (BiC) targets at the same revenue level, this document isolates the specific structural failures that drove profitability below target.

**Update (v2.2):** This document has been updated to reflect the correct EBITDA normalization methodology (partners are compensated at or below FMV, so no add-back is applied) and the separation of RepScheduler and PulseOne Communications costs from the operating baseline.

## D. The Profit Leakage Waterfall
A BiC company at $5,168,754 in revenue would generate approximately **$904,531 (17.5%)** in normalized EBITDA. PulseOne generated **$326,275 (6.3%)**. 

The gap to BiC is **$578,256**. Here is exactly where that profit leaked:

### 1. Project Margin Collapse: ~$303,159 Leakage
The single largest structural failure is the Consulting/Project division. 
- PulseOne generated $1,175,036 in consulting revenue at a **24.2% Gross Margin**.
- BiC target for project work is **50.0%**.
- The gap is driven almost entirely by subcontractor pass-through costs consuming 58 cents of every consulting dollar. If projects had been priced or staffed to achieve 50% margin, PulseOne would have retained an additional $303K in gross profit.

### 2. Sales & Marketing Overspend: ~$79,732 Leakage
PulseOne spent heavily on marketing that failed to produce growth.
- Corrected S&M spend (after removing RepScheduler development costs) was **$358,844 (6.9% of revenue)**.
- BiC target is **5.4%** ($279,112).
- You overspent the BiC model by ~$80K to produce a 16.2% *decline* in Managed Services revenue. The issue here is not just the $80K overspend, but the complete ROI failure of the base $279K.

### 3. SaaS Tool Sprawl: ~$91,000 Leakage
The G&A overhead gap is largely explained by redundant software tools.
- 49 vendors are present in the Computer & Internet Expenses line.
- Previous analysis identified approximately $91K in redundant or overlapping licenses that could be rationalized without impacting service delivery.

### 4. Unidentified / Other G&A: ~$104,365 Leakage
The remaining gap consists of general administrative inefficiencies, likely scattered across professional fees, travel, and non-delivery administrative payroll.

## E. What is NOT the Problem
The forensic analysis clears several areas that might typically be suspected of dragging down margins:

1. **Managed Services Delivery:** The core MSP delivery engine (excluding projects) is operating at a **56.0% Gross Margin**, which exceeds the BiC 52% target. The help desk technicians are highly efficient.
2. **Comprehensive Services (ARC):** The staff augmentation contract operated at a **48.0% Gross Margin**, which is highly profitable for that business model.
3. **Partner Compensation:** At $423K total, the partners are not over-extracting cash relative to the market value of their executive roles.

## F. Summary Conclusion
The 6.3% EBITDA result is not a generalized failure across the business. The core managed services engine is healthy. The $578K profit gap is highly concentrated in three specific, fixable areas:

1. Subcontractor-heavy project delivery that destroys margin.
2. A marketing apparatus that consumes cash without generating pipeline.
3. Unmanaged SaaS vendor sprawl.

Fixing these three structural issues would close 82% of the gap to Best-in-Class profitability.
