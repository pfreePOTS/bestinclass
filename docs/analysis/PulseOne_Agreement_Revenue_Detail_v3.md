## Critical Findings

**1. PulseOne's core Managed Services Agreement production is almost zero.**
When we separate out Arc Research Institute (Comprehensive Services) and IT Assessments, the true "Managed Services Agreement" category yielded only **$13,990** in all of 2025 across the entire sales team. While 2026 is pacing slightly better ($18K annualized), this indicates that the company essentially does not have a functioning engine for selling standard recurring managed services.

**2. IT Assessments are not converting.**
PulseOne sold $53,462 in IT Assessments in 2025 (led by CWiggins and MMoreira) and $19,240 YTD in 2026 (led by MMoreira). While these are profitable projects (50-51% margin), they are failing to translate into Managed Services Agreements. MMoreira sold 10 assessments in 2025 and 7 in 2026, but only closed $4.9K and $2.5K in MS Agreements respectively.

**3. eanzalone's production was distorted by one client.**
The $178K "Comprehensive Services" line item was entirely driven by Arc Research Institute. Without that client, eanzalone's true MS Agreement production in 2025 was only $5,865. In 2026, without Arc Research, their production consists entirely of small adjustments to existing clients.
