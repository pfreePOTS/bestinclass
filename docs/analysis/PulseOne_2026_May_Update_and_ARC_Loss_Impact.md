## C. The Revenue Shock: April 2026

The loss of the ARC Research contract represents a massive structural shock to the business, and the impact became fully visible in the April 2026 financials.

### 1. The ARC Impact
The ARC staff augmentation contract was driving approximately $84,000 per month in highly profitable revenue [1]. The Sales Review Report confirms that the "Project Revenue - Agreements" line, which averaged $115,079 from January through March, dropped to zero starting in April.

Consequently, total monthly revenue collapsed from a Q1 average of ~$413,000 down to **$301,523** in April [1]. 

### 2. Managed Services (MS) Churn
Beyond ARC, the core Managed Services base is also shrinking. The MS recurring revenue base ended 2025 at $145,911 per month. Despite winning 12 new accounts or upgrades (totaling +$7,044/mo), the business suffered severe churn [1]:
*   **Bunzl Agreement Reduction:** -$5,000/mo
*   **"Act" Leaving:** -$5,730/mo
*   **Conejo Leaving (May):** -$3,770/mo
*   **Sea Ranch Leaving (July):** -$2,500/mo

The net result is that the core MS base is projected to drop to **$136,020 per month** by June 2026—a net decline of nearly $10,000 per month (-$112K annualized) compared to the end of 2025 [1].

### 3. Immediate Profitability Impact
The April snapshot in the Sales Review Report paints a stark picture. With revenue down to $301K and total payroll expenses across all platforms running at $131K, the business operated at a **Net Cash deficit of -$27,513** for the month [1]. Even if projected project revenue materializes, the expected net margin for April is just 1.9% ($5,901).
