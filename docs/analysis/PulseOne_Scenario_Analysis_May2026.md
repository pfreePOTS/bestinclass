## A. Objective
To evaluate PulseOne's forward-looking profitability across three realistic revenue scenarios based on the volume of Standard invoicing (Projects, T&M, ad-hoc work). This analysis isolates the fixed recurring revenue base and demonstrates how the highly variable Standard invoicing dictates cash flow and EBITDA.

**Fixed Revenue Baseline: $227,300/month**

- Comprehensive Services (Bunzl MX): $8,600

**Fixed Operating Expenses: $132,764/month**

*Note: All W-2 and contracted service delivery labor is treated as a fixed cost, as these resources are retained regardless of month-to-month project volume.*

## D. Breakeven Analysis

To cover the fixed $132,764 in operating expenses, PulseOne must generate sufficient Gross Margin from both the fixed baseline and variable Standard invoicing.

- **Current Fixed Gross Margin:** $67,290
- **Gap to cover OpEx:** $65,474
- **Required Standard Invoicing (at 80% margin):**
  - For EBITDA Breakeven ($0): **$81,842 / month**
  - For Cash Breakeven (covering $20K D&D): **$106,842 / month**

**Total Revenue Required:**
- EBITDA Breakeven: **$309,142 / month**
- Cash Breakeven: **$334,142 / month**

*Note: April's actual Standard invoicing was approximately $110K (driving total revenue to $310K), placing the company slightly above cash breakeven for that specific month. However, if Standard invoicing drops to $25K-$50K, the company immediately burns $45K-$65K in cash.*

### Conclusion
PulseOne is structurally reliant on $80K-$100K/month in variable Standard invoicing just to break even. Because the internal project labor is a fixed cost, low project months result in catastrophic margins (-77%), while high project months yield acceptable margins (40%). To stabilize the business, management must either guarantee $100K+/month in Standard invoicing or execute the identified $23K+ in Sales/G&A cuts to lower the breakeven point to a sustainable $280K/month.
