# Service Leadership Best in Class (BIC) Framework Reference

This document serves as a working reference for the Service Leadership Best in Class (BIC) framework, compiled from publicly available Service Leadership Index® reports, ConnectWise publications, and industry analyses. It is designed to evaluate IT Solution Providers (TSPs) and Managed Service Providers (MSPs) against top-performing peers.

## 1. Core Definitions and Methodology

The Service Leadership Index® evaluates IT solution providers across 104 countries, categorizing them by Predominant Business Model™ (PBM™). A firm's PBM is determined by the largest segment of its service revenue (e.g., Infrastructure-Managed Services, Value-Added Reseller) [1] [2].

**Best in Class (BIC)** is defined exclusively as those firms performing in the **top quartile (top 25%) of Adjusted EBITDA percentage** within their specific PBM, after accounting for owner fair market compensation [1] [3]. 

From 2008 through 2015, and continuing through recent data, BIC MSPs consistently deliver roughly **2.5 to 3 times higher bottom-line profitability** than median-performing MSPs (e.g., 19.1% vs. 7.4% EBITDA) [1] [4]. In 2024, the industry saw a record fifth consecutive year of 19%+ adjusted EBITDA profitability for BIC TSPs [5].

## 2. Operational Maturity Levels (OML™)

Financial performance is heavily correlated with a firm's Operational Maturity Level™ (OML™). Service Leadership evaluates OML across five functional areas: Strategy, Finance, Sales, Service, and Compensation [4].

There are five levels of operational maturity:
*   **OML 1 (Beginning):** Characterized by low to negative financial performance, high chaos, and inconsistent service quality.
*   **OML 2 (Emerging):** Slight improvements in consistency, but financial performance remains below median.
*   **OML 3 (Scaling):** The firm achieves roughly median financial performance. Processes are consistent enough to scale, but not yet optimized for high profitability.
*   **OML 4 (Optimizing):** The firm begins achieving BIC (top quartile) financial performance through high efficiency, standardized technology stacks, and strong value creation.
*   **OML 5 (Innovating):** The highest level of financial performance and service quality. These firms often add advanced or non-IT services in a highly controlled, profitable manner [4].

Data shows a strong correlation: reaching an OML of 3.8 or higher typically results in BIC profitability [3].

## 3. The Importance of a Reference Architecture

A critical driver of OML and BIC profitability is the deployment and strict enforcement of a **Reference Architecture** [1]. 

A Reference Architecture is a single, standardized design spanning the entire technology stack (networks, servers, endpoints, security, BCDR) that the MSP supports. BIC MSPs enforce this standard across the vast majority of their client base [1].

Benefits of a strict Reference Architecture include:
*   **Reduced Complexity:** Narrows the technology stack the "Service Factory" must support, dramatically lowering service Cost of Goods Sold (COGS).
*   **Higher Service Margins:** Less exception handling leads to higher technician utilization and efficiency.
*   **Lower SG&A:** A standardized offering requires less custom quoting and engineering per sale, lowering Sales and General & Administrative costs.
*   **Increased Win Rates:** A clearly defined, specialized offering attracts clients willing to pay for value, shortening sales cycles and increasing the average contract value [1].

## 4. Key Financial Benchmarks (Infrastructure-Managed Services PBM)

The following benchmarks represent the performance of Best-in-Class MSPs compared to Median and Bottom Quartile performers. 

### Profitability and Margins

| Metric | Bottom Quartile | Median | Best-in-Class (BIC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Adjusted EBITDA %** | -1.3% to 0% | 8.3% to 10.3% | **18.3% to 19.0%+** |
| **Blended Gross Margin %** | 35.3% | 38.3% | **44.0%+** |
| **Managed Services Gross Margin %** | 41.7% | 46.1% | **48.7% to 52.4%** |
| **Product Gross Margin %** | 19.9% | 22.6% | **24.3% to 26.3%** |
| **Project/Professional Services GM %** | < 12.9% | ~19.4% | **> 24.6%** |

*Note: 2024 and 2025 data indicates BIC Managed Services Gross Margins frequently exceed 48%, with top performers reaching 52.4% [1] [6] [7]. Product margins for top performers sit around 24-26% [1] [8].*

### Operating Expenses (SG&A)

Controlling Selling, General, and Administrative (SG&A) expenses is vital. SG&A should be measured as a percentage of gross margin dollars. Best-in-class MSPs typically spend only **$0.58 of every $1.00 in gross margin on SG&A**, leaving $0.42 for profit or reinvestment [9].

| Metric | Bottom Quartile | Median | Best-in-Class (BIC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total SG&A Cost %** | 37.2% | 31.4% | **27.4%** |
| **Sales Cost %** | 7.6% | 6.8% | **5.4%** |
| **G&A Cost %** | 30.1% | 25.1% | **22.6%** |

*Efficiency Indicator: BIC firms generate **$8.21 in Gross Margin** for every $1 spent on sales and marketing, compared to $5.59 for median firms [1].*

### Growth and Valuation

*   **Revenue Growth:** BIC MSPs typically sustain total revenue growth of 16.3% or higher, with Adjusted EBITDA dollar growth exceeding 25% [6].
*   **Valuation Multiples:** Due to high profitability and growth, valuation multiples for BIC TSPs reached near-record highs in 2024, increasing approximately 22% from 2023 [5].
*   **Service Multiple of Labor:** BIC firms achieve a service multiple of labor of **2.8x or higher** [6].

## 5. Client and Employee Retention

High Operational Maturity drives better experiences for both clients and staff.

*   **Client Retention:** BIC MSPs typically maintain client retention rates above 76% (less than 24% churn), compared to industry averages around 73% [10].
*   **Employee Turnover:** BIC firms experience significantly lower employee turnover, averaging around **11.9%**, whereas rates above 15% indicate cultural or operational issues common in lower OML firms [6] [11].
*   **Customer Satisfaction:** BIC firms report Net Promoter Scores (NPS) averaging 68.7, compared to 51.2 for bottom quartile firms [6].
