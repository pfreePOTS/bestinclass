# Service Leadership Research Notes

## Source 1: Service Leadership First Principles (ConnectWise Blog, 3/31/2026)
- BIC = Top Quartile of EBITDA% within a given PBM
- BIC firms earn ~2.5x higher EBITDA% than median peers
- 25% of all IT solution providers attain BIC
- Strong correlation: OML 3.8+ typically = BIC profitability
- OML 3.0+ = marked EBITDA improvement and consistency
- Normalized Solution Provider Chart of Accounts is the standard measurement tool
- Service gross margin is a PRIMARY driver of profitability
- Key mistakes: not pricing appropriately, not raising prices, hiring too many COGS people, not enough margin in product sales, too much SG&A
- VCS (Value Creation Strategy) = paramount OML trait
- PBMs: different business models have different inherent financial drivers and different BIC ratios

## Source 2: Auvik / MSP KB - OML Definitions
- OML 1 (Beginning): Low to negative financial performance, inconsistent service quality
- OML 2 (Emerging): Improving but still inconsistent
- OML 3 (Scaling): Consistent processes, beginning to scale
- OML 4 (Optimizing): High performance, refined processes
- OML 5 (Innovating): Highest financial performance, highest value/quality services

## Source 3: Various LinkedIn / Industry Sources
- BIC MSPs achieve 19%+ adjusted EBITDA
- OML 5 = ~2x profitability vs OML 3, ~4x vs OML 1
- Revenue tiers affect EBITDA stability and valuation multiples
